# Complete Lecture: Learning Approach (Cambridge International AS & A Level Psychology 9990)

The learning approach explains behavior as acquired through experience, conditioning, and observation. For qualification context see [Cambridge 9990](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-psychology-9990/).

---

## 1. Core assumptions

- Behavior is largely learned from the environment.
- Learning follows lawful processes that can be studied scientifically.
- Complex behavior can be built from simpler associations and consequences.

---

## 2. Classical conditioning

Classical conditioning links a previously neutral stimulus to a reflex response.

Key terms:
- **Unconditioned stimulus (UCS)** naturally produces a response.
- **Unconditioned response (UCR)** is the natural reflex.
- **Conditioned stimulus (CS)** starts neutral but gains power after pairing.
- **Conditioned response (CR)** is the learned response to the CS.

Processes:
- Acquisition through repeated pairing.
- Extinction if CS is repeatedly presented without UCS.
- Spontaneous recovery can occur after a rest interval.

---

## 3. Operant conditioning

Operant conditioning explains behavior through consequences.

- **Positive reinforcement:** add a desirable consequence.
- **Negative reinforcement:** remove an unpleasant condition.
- **Punishment:** reduce behavior by adding/removing outcomes.

Students should distinguish reinforcement (increases behavior) from punishment (decreases behavior).

---

## 4. Social learning and observational learning

People learn through models, especially when:
- Model is high status, similar, or rewarded.
- Observer pays attention and can reproduce behavior.
- There is motivation to imitate.

This bridges strict behaviorism and cognition because mental processes mediate imitation.

---

## 5. Applications

Learning principles are used in:
- Behavior modification programs.
- Education and classroom management.
- Habit change and token economies.
- Exposure-based interventions for anxiety.

Applied examples gain marks when linked explicitly to concepts like reinforcement schedules or stimulus generalization.

---

## 6. Evaluation in essays

**Strengths:**
- Objective, testable theories.
- Practical usefulness across settings.

**Limitations:**
- May ignore biological predispositions.
- Sometimes reductionist for complex social behavior.
- Lab research can lack ecological validity.

Use evaluative language: "This suggests...", "However...", "A methodological issue is...".

---

## Common misconceptions

- Negative reinforcement is not punishment.
- Observational learning does not require direct reward every time.
- Learned behavior can become automatic but still originated through experience.
