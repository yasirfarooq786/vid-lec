# Complete Lecture: Cognitive Approach (Cambridge International AS & A Level Psychology 9990)

The cognitive approach focuses on internal mental processes such as perception, attention, memory, language, and thinking. For qualification context see [Cambridge 9990](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-psychology-9990/).

---

## 1. Core assumptions

- Humans are active information processors.
- Behavior is influenced by how information is encoded, stored, and retrieved.
- Mental processes can be studied indirectly through controlled experiments.

---

## 2. Information processing model

Typical flow:
1. **Input** from environment.
2. **Processing** by attention and interpretation.
3. **Storage** in memory systems.
4. **Output** as behavior or verbal report.

This model supports measurable variables such as reaction time, recall accuracy, and error patterns.

---

## 3. Memory systems

Students should explain distinctions between:
- **Sensory memory** (very brief register).
- **Short-term/working memory** (limited capacity and duration).
- **Long-term memory** (large capacity, variable retrieval).

Useful terminology:
- Encoding, storage, retrieval.
- Interference, forgetting, reconstruction.

---

## 4. Schemas and reconstructive memory

**Schemas** are mental frameworks built from past experience.

Benefits:
- Fast interpretation.
- Efficient decision-making.

Limitations:
- Can distort recall.
- Can increase bias and stereotyping.

Many past-paper questions reward students who connect schema theory to real-world errors in eyewitness memory or social judgment.

---

## 5. Cognitive biases and decision making

Examples include:
- Confirmation bias.
- Availability bias.
- Framing effects.

In essays, link bias to everyday outcomes and evaluate method quality of supporting studies.

---

## 6. Research methods and evaluation

**Strengths:**
- High control in lab tasks.
- Quantifiable dependent variables.
- Practical applications in education and legal settings.

**Limitations:**
- Inference problem: we infer mental processes from behavior.
- Artificial tasks may reduce ecological validity.
- Cultural context can influence cognitive strategies.

---

## 7. Building high-mark answers

For 9990 responses:
1. Define the cognitive concept accurately.
2. Describe one supporting piece of evidence.
3. Evaluate with both methodological and conceptual points.
4. Apply to the scenario in the question stem.

---

## Common misconceptions

- Cognitive approach does not ignore biology; modern explanations often integrate both.
- Better recall is not always more accurate recall.
- Correlation between confidence and memory accuracy is not guaranteed.
