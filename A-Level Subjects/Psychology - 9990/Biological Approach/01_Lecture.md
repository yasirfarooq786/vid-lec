# Complete Lecture: Biological Approach (Cambridge International AS & A Level Psychology 9990)

The biological approach explains behavior through the brain, nervous system, hormones, and genetics. For syllabus context see [Cambridge 9990](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-psychology-9990/).

---

## 1. Core assumptions

- Human behavior has a physical basis in the body and brain.
- Psychological differences can be linked to differences in brain structure, chemistry, and inherited traits.
- Behavior is influenced by evolution: traits that improved survival were more likely to be passed on.

---

## 2. The nervous system and endocrine system

**Central nervous system (CNS):**
- Brain and spinal cord.
- Processes information and coordinates responses.

**Peripheral nervous system (PNS):**
- Connects CNS to the rest of the body.
- Includes the **somatic** and **autonomic** systems.

**Endocrine system:**
- Glands release hormones into blood (for example cortisol, adrenaline, testosterone, oxytocin).
- Hormonal effects are usually slower than neural transmission but longer-lasting.

---

## 3. Brain and behavior

Students should be able to explain broad functions of key regions:
- **Frontal lobe:** planning, decision-making, inhibition.
- **Temporal lobe:** memory and language processing.
- **Parietal lobe:** sensory integration and spatial processing.
- **Occipital lobe:** visual processing.

Linking structure to behavior is often tested through evidence from injury, scans, or clinical cases.

---

## 4. Neurochemistry and neurotransmitters

Behavior and emotion are influenced by signaling chemicals:
- **Serotonin** and mood regulation.
- **Dopamine** and reward/motivation.
- **Noradrenaline** and arousal/stress.

Exam responses should avoid over-simplified claims like "one neurotransmitter causes one disorder." Strong answers discuss interaction between biology, environment, and cognition.

---

## 5. Genetics and heritability

- **Genes** are inherited units of DNA.
- **Genotype** refers to genetic makeup; **phenotype** is observable outcome.
- **Heritability estimates** indicate how much variation in a trait in a population can be attributed to genetic differences.

Twin and adoption studies support genetic influence, but they do not prove total genetic determination.

---

## 6. Biological research methods

Common methods include:
- Brain imaging techniques.
- Laboratory experiments on physiological variables.
- Correlational and family studies.

Evaluation points:
- **Strengths:** objective measures, scientific credibility, potential applications to treatment.
- **Limitations:** reductionism, ethical issues, and difficulty separating nature from nurture.

---

## 7. How to evaluate in 9990 essays

Use balanced paragraphs:
1. Explain the biological claim clearly.
2. Support with named evidence/study findings.
3. Evaluate with methodological and conceptual criticism.
4. Link back to the question.

---

## Common misconceptions

- Biological explanation does not mean behavior is fixed forever.
- A correlation between brain activity and behavior does not always show cause.
- Genetic influence is probabilistic, not a guaranteed outcome.
