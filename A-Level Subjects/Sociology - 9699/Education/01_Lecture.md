# Complete Lecture: Education (Cambridge International AS & A Level Sociology 9699)

This lecture covers **purposes of education**, **achievement and inequality**, **school processes**, and **theory**—core material for **9699**. Programme: [Cambridge 9699](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-sociology-9699/).

---

## 1. Education and society

Education is analysed as:
- **Socialisation** (norms, values, roles).
- **Selection** and **allocation** (credentials, sorting into work).
- **Social control** (discipline, surveillance).
- **Reproduction** or **challenge** of inequality (theoretical debate).

---

## 2. Theoretical approaches (essential contrasts)

**Functionalism (Durkheim, Parsons—overview):**
- Schools transmit **shared values** (**value consensus**) and **specialist skills**.
- **Meritocracy** as an ideal—**evaluate** whether real societies match this.

**Marxism:**
- **Correspondence** ideas: school mirrors workplace authority; **hidden curriculum**; **credential inflation** debates.
- Education may **legitimate** inequality (ideology).

**Feminism:**
- Gendered expectations, subjects, and outcomes; **patriarchy** in curriculum and interaction.

**Interactionism:**
- **Labelling**, **self-fulfilling prophecy**, **pupil subcultures**, teacher expectations—micro-level processes shaping outcomes.

---

## 3. Class, ethnicity, and gender (achievement)

Exam questions often require **multi-factor** explanations:
- **Material** factors (resources, housing, health).
- **Cultural** factors (language, aspirations, parental education—discuss **carefully**, avoid deficit stereotypes).
- **School processes** (setting/streaming, discipline, teacher expectations).
- **Policy** (comprehensives, marketisation debates where relevant).

Always **balance** structure and agency; use **evidence** cautiously and **evaluate** bias in sources.

---

## 4. Marketisation and policy debates (where syllabus includes)

Themes may include: competition between schools, selection, privatisation pressures, inequality effects—link to **Marxist** / **New Right** style arguments and **evaluate** with evidence.

---

## 5. Writing strong answers

- **Define** meritocracy, hidden curriculum, labelling—precisely.
- **Chain** mechanisms: e.g. label → teacher response → pupil identity → attainment.
- **Compare** at least two perspectives on the same issue (e.g. role of education in mobility).

---

## Common misconceptions

- Correlation between parental income and attainment is **not** purely “ability.”
- **Cultural deprivation** arguments are **contested**—present as debate, not fact.
- Interactionism explains **process**; it does not deny wider inequality.
