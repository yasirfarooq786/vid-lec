# Complete Lecture: Theory and Methods (Cambridge International AS & A Level Sociology 9699)

This lecture integrates **social theory** (ways of seeing society) and **research methods** (how sociologists produce knowledge)—essential for high marks across **9699**. Programme: [Cambridge 9699](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-sociology-9699/).

## 1. Positivism vs interpretivism (overview)

- **Positivism:** society like natural systems; favour **quantitative**, measurable data; law-like generalisations (debated).
- **Interpretivism:** social reality is **meaningful**; favour **qualitative** methods to understand actors’ viewpoints.

**Practical point:** methods are chosen to match **aims**—no single “best” method.

---

## 2. Major theoretical perspectives (exam shorthand)

- **Functionalism:** consensus, institutions, social order, **functions** and **dysfunctions**.
- **Marxism:** conflict, capitalism, **ideology**, **class** relations.
- **Feminism:** patriarchy, gendered structures; multiple strands.
- **Interactionism / social action:** meanings, labelling, everyday interaction.
- **Postmodernism** (where relevant): fragmentation, media, **metanarratives** questioned—use precisely, avoid vague waffle.

---

## 3. Research methods (core toolkit)

**Quantitative:**
- **Questionnaires/surveys:** large **N**, comparability—risk of **validity** (meaning of answers), **response rate**.
- **Structured interviews:** standardised—control vs rapport limits.
- **Official statistics:** cheap, longitudinal potential—**construction** issues.

**Qualitative:**
- **Unstructured/semi-structured interviews:** depth, meanings—**generalisation** limits, **researcher effect**.
- **Participant observation:** insider access—**ethical** and **access** issues, subjectivity.
- **Documents/media content analysis:** historical/media topics—coding reliability matters.

---

## 4. Key concepts in evaluation

- **Validity** (truthfulness to concept), **reliability** (repeatability), **representativeness** (sampling).
- **Ethics:** consent, confidentiality, harm, vulnerable groups.
- **Triangulation:** combining methods to strengthen claims—still not perfect.

---

## 5. Sampling (basics)

- **Random**, **stratified**, **quota**, **snowball**—each suits different populations and aims.
- **Generalisation** depends on sampling and method fit.

---

## 6. Linking theory to method

Exam questions often reward:
- Functionalists/Marxists sometimes using **macro** quantitative patterns.
- Interactionists privileging **micro** qualitative insight.
- Showing **limitations** of a method for a given topic (e.g. observation of illegal activity).

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Objectivity** is not automatic with numbers—choices in design shape results.
- Qualitative research is **not** “less scientific”—it answers different questions.
- A theory is not “wrong” because one study contradicts it—discuss **scope** and **evidence quality**.
