# Complete Lecture: Past Papers and Examination Technique (Cambridge International AS & A Level Sociology 9699)

This module is **exam technique** for **9699**: command words, essay structure, use of evidence, and time management—not a substitute for the substantive topic lectures. Programme: [Cambridge 9699](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-sociology-9699/).

---

## 1. What past papers train

- Accuracy on **command words** (define, describe, explain, assess, evaluate, to what extent).
- **Theoretical** naming and **comparison**.
- **Application** to unseen scenarios (education, crime, media, etc.).
- **Evaluation** that goes beyond “this has limitations.”

---

## 2. Command words (typical patterns)

- **Define:** short, precise, sociological definition; avoid story.
- **Describe:** clear features or sequence; link to question focus.
- **Explain:** mechanisms and reasons—**because** chains.
- **Assess / Evaluate / To what extent:** balanced arguments + **judgement** tied to the question.

---

## 3. Essay structure that works

1. **Introduction:** define key terms + line of argument (two-sided if required).
2. **Body paragraphs:** **PEEL**-style—**point**, **evidence/example**, **explanation**, **link** back.
3. **Evaluation:** per paragraph or integrated section—method limits, counter-theory, scope.
4. **Conclusion:** answer the **exact** question—no new theory; summarise weight of evidence.

---

## 4. Using theory well

- **Name** the perspective in the first sentence of the paragraph.
- Avoid “word salad” of theorists—**depth beats name-dropping**.
- **Compare** on the same issue (e.g. Marxism vs functionalism on education).

---

## 5. Evidence without drift

- Use **trends** or **recognised studies** when relevant—**briefly**, tied to the claim.
- Do not narrate a study for half a page unless the question asks for it.

---

## 6. Timing and marks

- Plan longer questions (brief plan: **perspectives + points**).
- Leave space to finish conclusions—unfinished essays lose disproportionate marks.

---

## 7. Mark scheme habits

After each timed attempt:
1. Mark with official criteria if available.
2. Highlight **missed evaluation**.
3. Rewrite **one** paragraph to “model” standard within 24 hours.

---

## Revision checklist

- [ ] Command-word drill: 5 minutes each on define / explain / assess.
- [ ] One full timed paper per fortnight in final stretch.
- [ ] Bank of 20 definitions across topics—accurate wording.
- [ ] Three essay plans you can reuse with different questions (family, education, crime).
