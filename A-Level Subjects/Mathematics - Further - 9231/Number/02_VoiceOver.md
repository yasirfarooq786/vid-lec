# Voice-Over Script: Number (Cambridge International AS & A Level Further Mathematics 9231)

**[Visual: Argand diagram — z = x + iy, r and θ]**

**Narrator:**  
**Further Mathematics** treats **complex numbers** as **complete** objects. **Modulus** is **distance** from the **origin**; **argument** is **angle** from the **positive real axis**. **Multiply** two **complex** numbers: **multiply** **moduli**, **add** **arguments**.

---

**[Visual: De Moivre — (cos θ + i sin θ)^n = cos nθ + i sin nθ]**

**Narrator:**  
**De Moivre** turns **powers** into **multiple angles**—perfect for **roots of unity** and **solving z^n equals a constant**. **nth roots** sit at **vertices** of a **regular polygon** on a **circle**.

---

**[Visual: Locus |z − 3| = 2 — circle centre 3]**

**Narrator:**  
**Mod z minus a equals r** is a **circle** **centre** **a**, **radius r**. **Mod z minus a equals mod z minus b** is the **perpendicular bisector** of **a** and **b**—**geometry** in the **Argand** plane.

---

**[Visual: Telescoping sum 1/(1×2) + 1/(2×3) + …]**

**Narrator:**  
**Method of differences** **rewrites** each term as **f of r minus f of r plus one**. **Middle** terms **cancel**, leaving **ends** only. Pair with **standard** **sum** formulas for **r**, **r squared**, **r cubed**.

---

**[Visual: Induction domino — P(1), P(k) ⇒ P(k+1)]**

**Narrator:**  
**Induction** proves **statements** for **all** **positive integers**. Show **base** case, **assume** **true** for **k**, **prove** for **k plus one**, **conclude**. **Algebra** in the **inductive** step must **cite** the **hypothesis**.

---

**Narrator:**  
**9231** expects **fluency** moving between **cartesian**, **mod–arg**, and **exponential** **i theta** forms. Next: **algebra** at **Further** depth.
