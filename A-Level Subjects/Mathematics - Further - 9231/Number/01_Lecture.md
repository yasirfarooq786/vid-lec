## 1. Complex numbers (fundamentals)

**z = x + iy** with **i² = −1**.

**Modulus:** **|z| = √(x² + y²)**  
**Argument:** **arg z** (principal value in **(−π, π]** or as specified)

**Polar / Euler form:** **z = r(cos θ + i sin θ) = re^(iθ)**

**Multiplication / division:** **moduli multiply/divide**; **arguments add/subtract**.

**De Moivre’s theorem:** **(cos θ + i sin θ)ⁿ = cos nθ + i sin nθ** → **n**th roots of unity / **roots** of **zⁿ = w**.

**Conjugate z\***: **(z₁z₂)\* = z₁\*z₂\***; **zz\* = |z|²** (real).

**Loci:** **|z − a| = r** (circle); **|z − a| = |z − b|** (perpendicular bisector); **arg(z − a) = α** (half-line).

## 2. Roots of polynomials (real coefficients)

**Non-real roots** occur in **conjugate pairs** if **coefficients** are **real**.

If **α** is root of **P(x) = 0**, **(x − α)** is **factor** (Fundamental theorem of algebra — **n** roots counting **multiplicity** in **ℂ**).

---

## 3. Summation notation (Σ)

**Σᵣ₌ₘⁿ f(r)** — **linear**ity: **Σ(af + bg) = aΣf + bΣg**.

**Standard results (learn / prove once):**

- **Σr = ½n(n+1)**  
- **Σr² = (1/6)n(n+1)(2n+1)**  
- **Σr³ = [½n(n+1)]²**

**Method of differences** for **Σ 1/[r(r+1)]** type — **telescoping**.

---

## 4. Proof techniques (number-flavoured)

- **Proof by contradiction** (e.g. **√2** irrational style problems).  
- **Proof by induction** for **Σ formulas** and **divisibility** (**statement** for **n = 1**, **assume** **n = k**, **deduce** **n = k+1**, **conclusion**).

---

## 5. Inequalities (Further level)

**AM–GM** for **positive** reals: **(a+b)/2 ≥ √(ab)** (extension to **n** numbers where examined).

**Modulus** inequalities on **ℂ** and **ℝ**; **Cauchy–Schwarz** awareness in **vector** form (see **Vectors** topic).

## Common mistakes

- **Argument** **branch** errors — **watch** **range** of **principal** **arg**.  
- **Induction:** **algebra** in **k → k+1** step **must** use **inductive hypothesis** **explicitly**.
