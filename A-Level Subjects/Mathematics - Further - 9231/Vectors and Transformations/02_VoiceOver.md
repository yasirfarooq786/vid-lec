# Voice-Over Script: Vectors and Transformations (Cambridge International AS & A Level Further Mathematics 9231)

**[Visual: Right-hand rule — a × b direction]**

**Narrator:**  
The **vector** or **cross** product **a cross b** is **perpendicular** to **both** **a** and **b**. Its **length** is **mod a mod b sine theta**—the **area** of the **parallelogram** spanned by **a** and **b**. **Order** matters: **b cross a** is the **negative**.

---

**[Visual: Parallelepiped — volume scalar triple product]**

**Narrator:**  
**a dot b cross c** gives the **signed** **volume** of the **box** built on **three** **edges** from one **vertex**. **Zero** means the **vectors** are **coplanar**.

---

**[Visual: Matrix multiplying column vector — grid transformation]**

**Narrator:**  
A **matrix** **acts** on **column** vectors. **Composition** of **maps** corresponds to **matrix** **multiplication**—**watch** **which** **map** applies **first** in your **notation**.

---

**[Visual: Av = λv — eigenvector on same line through origin]**

**Narrator:**  
An **eigenvector** of **A** is a **non-zero** direction **unchanged** by **A**—only **scaled** by **lambda**. **Solve** **det of A minus lambda I equals zero** for **eigenvalues**, then **substitute** back for **eigenvectors**.

---

**[Visual: Diagonalisation A = PDP⁻¹]**

**Narrator:**  
If you have **enough** **independent** **eigenvectors**, **A** **diagonalises**: **P** holds **eigenvectors**, **D** holds **eigenvalues**. **Powers** of **A** become **easy** on the **diagonal** **side**.
