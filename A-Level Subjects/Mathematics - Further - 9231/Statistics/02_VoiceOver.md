# Voice-Over Script: Statistics (Cambridge International AS & A Level Further Mathematics 9231)

**[Visual: PDF curve — shaded area P(a ≤ X ≤ b)]**

**Narrator:**  
**Continuous** **random** variables use **density** **functions**. **Probability** is **area** under the **curve**, **not** the **height** at a **point**. **Total** **area** must be **one**.

---

**[Visual: X̄ sampling distribution narrowing with n]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sample means** **vary less** than **individual** **observations**. If the **population** is **normal**, **X bar** is **exactly** **normal** with **variance** **sigma squared over n**. **Central Limit Theorem** says **large** samples make **X bar** **approximately** **normal** even when **data** **aren’t**.

---

**[Visual: t-distribution vs normal — heavier tails]**

**Narrator:**  
When **sigma** is **unknown**, replace it by **sample s** and use **t** **critical** values—**wider** **intervals** because **estimating** **spread** adds **uncertainty**. **Degrees of freedom** are **n minus one** for a **one-sample** **mean**.

---

**[Visual: χ² table — observed vs expected cells]**

**Narrator:**  
**Chi-squared** tests **compare** **observed** counts to **expected** counts under a **model** or **independence**. **Small** **expected** counts **invalidate** the **approximation**—**merge** **categories** if needed.

---

**[Visual: H0 vs H1 — tails shaded for rejection]**

**Narrator:**  
**Hypothesis** testing balances **errors**. **Rejecting** a **true** null is **Type one**—**controlled** by **alpha**. **Failing** to **reject** a **false** null is **Type two**—**power** is **one minus beta**.
