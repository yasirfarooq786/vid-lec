# Voice-Over Script: Coordinate Geometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Further Mathematics 9231)

**[Visual: Polar grid — point (r, θ) marked]**

**Narrator:**  
**Polar** coordinates use **distance r** from the **pole** and **angle theta** from the **initial line**. **Spirals**, **cardioids**, and **roses** are **natural** in **polar** form—**sketch** by **sampling** **theta**.

---

**[Visual: Thin polar sector — dA = ½ r² dθ]**

**Narrator:**  
**Area** inside a **polar** curve integrates **one half r squared d theta**. It is the **limit** of **little** **sectors**, **not** **rectangular** **strips**.

---

**[Visual: Parametric curve — particle at (f(t), g(t))]**

**Narrator:**  
**Parametric** equations **drive** **x** and **y** from a **parameter** **t**—think **time**. **dy by dx** is **dy by dt** over **dx by dt**. For **curvature** or **second** derivative, **differentiate** **again** with respect to **t**, **chain** carefully.

---

**[Visual: Ellipse x²/a² + y²/b² = 1 — foci]**

**Narrator:**  
**Conic** sections appear as **slice** **sections** of a **cone**. The **ellipse** keeps **sum** of **distances** to **foci** **constant**. **Hyperbola** uses **difference**; **parabola** matches **distance** to **focus** and **directrix**.
