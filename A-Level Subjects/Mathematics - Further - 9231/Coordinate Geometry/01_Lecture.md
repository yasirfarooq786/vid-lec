## 1. Polar coordinates

**Point** **(r, θ)** with **r ≥ 0** (usually), **θ** measured from **initial line** (positive **x**-axis).

**Conversion:** **x = r cos θ**, **y = r sin θ**  
**r = √(x² + y²)**, **tan θ = y/x** (**quadrant** care).

**Curve** **r = f(θ)** — **sketch** by **plotting** **key** **θ**; **negative** **r** interpretation (**θ + π**) if **allowed**.

---

## 2. Area in polar form

**Area bounded** by **r = f(θ)** from **θ = α** to **β:**

**A = ½ ∫[α,β] r² dθ**

**Derived** from **sector** **elements** **½ r² δθ**.

---

## 3. Parametric equations

**x = f(t), y = g(t)** — **eliminate** **t** for **Cartesian** equation; **dy/dx = (dy/dt)/(dx/dt)** (**chain** rule).

**Second derivative:** **d²y/dx² = d/dx(dy/dx) = (d/dt(dy/dx)) / (dx/dt)**

---

## 4. Conic sections (standard forms)

**Parabola:** **y² = 4ax** (focus **(a,0)**, directrix **x = −a**)

**Ellipse:** **x²/a² + y²/b² = 1** (**semi-axes** **a, b**)

**Hyperbola:** **x²/a² − y²/b² = 1** (**asymptotes** **y = ±(b/a)x**)

**Eccentricity** **e** and **focus–directrix** property — use **syllabus** **definitions**.

**Tangents** / **normals** via **implicit** or **parametric** **differentiation**.

---

## 5. Cartesian geometry (Further applications)

**Line** in **3D** (**vector** form) — see **Vectors** topic.

**General second-degree** **Ax² + Bxy + Cy² + … = 0** — **rotation** of **axes** to **remove** **xy** (**optional** **depth**).

## Common mistakes

- **Polar** **area** using **wrong** **limits** — **trace** **curve** once.  
- **Parametric** **d²y/dx²** — **not** simply **(d²y/dt²)/(d²x/dt²)**.
