# Voice-Over Script: Trigonometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Further Mathematics 9231)

**[Visual: Graphs sin, arcsin — restricted domain highlighted]**

**Narrator:**  
**Inverse** **sine** only exists as a **function** once you **restrict** **sine** to a **one-one** **interval**. **Principal** values live in **closed minus half pi to half pi** for **arc sine**, **zero to pi** for **arc cosine**—**match** your **formula booklet**.

---

**[Visual: Derivative 1/sqrt(1−x²) as gradient of arcsin]**

**Narrator:**  
**Differentiating** **inverse** **trig** uses **implicit** **differentiation** on **y equals arc sine x**. Results appear in the **booklet**, but **chain** rule **inside** is still **your** job.

---

**[Visual: sin P + sin Q formula transforming to product]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sum-to-product** formulas turn **sums** of **sines** into **products**—ideal for **integrating** **sin mx cos nx** or **solving** **harmonic** **equations**.

---

**[Visual: Side-by-side sin/cos and sinh/cosh graphs]**

**Narrator:**  
**Hyperbolic** functions **mirror** **trig** identities with **strategic** **sign** **flips**. **Cosh** is **even** and **at least one**; **sinh** is **odd** and **passes** through **origin**.

---

**[Visual: t = tan(θ/2) substitution schematic]**

**Narrator:**  
The **Weierstrass** **substitution** **t equals tan half theta** **linearises** **rational** combinations of **sine** and **cosine**—a **Further** **integration** **power** move.
