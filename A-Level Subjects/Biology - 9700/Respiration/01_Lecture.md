## 12.1 Energy in living organisms

### Why cells need energy

- **Anabolic** processes (biosynthesis), **active transport**, **movement**, **cell division**, **nerve** activity, **maintenance** of **body temperature** (in endotherms) all require **usable** energy currency.

### ATP

- **Adenosine triphosphate:** **adenine + ribose + three phosphates**.
- **Hydrolysis** of terminal **phosphate** bond releases energy (often coupled via enzymes): **ATP → ADP + Pi** (approximately **30.5 kJ mol⁻¹** in textbook values—use figures given in your exam).
- **Rephosphorylation** links **catabolic** pathways (respiration) to **ATP synthase** activity.

**Exam tip:** State that energy is **released** when **bonds are broken** in a **specific context**—examiners prefer **“exergonic hydrolysis coupled to cellular work”** over vague “energy stored in bonds.”

## 12.2 Respiration — overview

**Aerobic respiration (summary):**  
**Glucose + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water** (+ **ATP**).

**Anaerobic respiration:** **No oxygen** as **final electron acceptor**; **partial** oxidation; **lower ATP yield**; **lactate** or **ethanol + CO₂** as products in common examples.

### Glycolysis (cytoplasm)

- **Glucose (6C)** → **two pyruvate (3C)**.
- **Net:** **2 ATP** (substrate-level), **2 NADH** (carries **reducing power** to mitochondria in aerobic conditions).
- **Anaerobic** or **aerobic**—glycolysis itself needs **no oxygen**.

### Link reaction (mitochondrial matrix)

- Each **pyruvate** → **acetyl CoA** + **CO₂**; **NAD** reduced to **NADH**.

### Krebs cycle (matrix)

- **Acetyl CoA** combines with **oxaloacetate** → **citrate**; series of oxidations/regenerations.
- **Outputs per glucose** (when counting both turns): **4 CO₂**, **reduced coenzymes** (**NADH**, **FADH₂**), small **ATP** (or **GTP**) via **substrate-level** phosphorylation (teaching counts vary—learn **your** specification’s tally).

### Oxidative phosphorylation (inner mitochondrial membrane / cristae)

- **Electron transport chain** passes electrons from **NADH/FADH₂** through **carriers**; energy releases **protons** into **intermembrane space**.
- **Chemiosmosis:** **proton gradient** drives **ATP synthase** → **ATP**; **oxygen** is **final electron acceptor** forming **water**.
- **Aerobic** respiration yields **many** more **ATP** than **glycolysis alone** (approximate totals such as **~30–32 ATP** per glucose are common in teaching—**exam papers** may use simplified **38** or **less**; **always justify** with **proton** and **shuttle** details if asked).

### Anaerobic pathways

- **Mammalian muscle (intense exercise):** **pyruvate → lactate** (regenerates **NAD** so **glycolysis** continues); **lactate** may be **oxidised** later or **converted** in **liver** (Cori cycle awareness).
- **Yeast / many plant tissues:** **pyruvate** decarboxylated to **ethanal** → **ethanol** + **CO₂** (**alcoholic fermentation**).

### Respiratory quotient (RQ)

**RQ = volume of CO₂ produced ÷ volume of O₂ consumed**

- **Carbohydrate** ≈ **1**; **lipid** **< 1**; **protein** intermediate.
- Used in **metabolic** and **respirometer** questions.

## Practical: respirometer / simple metabolic experiments

- **Soda lime** or **KOH** absorbs **CO₂** → measure **O₂ consumption** by **volume** change.
- **Control:** **thermostat** (temperature affects rate), **same mass** of organism (or **dry mass**), **acclimatisation**, **dark** for **plants** to avoid **photosynthesis** cancelling **CO₂** release.
- **Deduce RQ** from **simultaneous** O₂ and CO₂ measurements when both recorded.

## Mitochondrial structure (linked to function)

- **Outer** and **inner** membrane; **cristae** increase **surface area** for **ETC** and **ATP synthase**.
- **Matrix** contains **Krebs** enzymes and **mitochondrial DNA** (from Topic 1).

## Common misconceptions

- **Respiration** is **not** “breathing”—it is **enzyme-catalysed** release of energy from **substrates**.
- **Anaerobic** does **not** mean “no ATP”—it means **no** **oxidative phosphorylation** using **oxygen**.
- **CO₂** is produced in **link** and **Krebs**, **not** in **glycolysis** (for glucose).
