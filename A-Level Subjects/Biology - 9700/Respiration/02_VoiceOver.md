# Voice-Over Script: Respiration (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Title — “9700 Topic 12: Energy and respiration” — mitochondrion]**

**Narrator:**  
**Respiration** transfers energy from **glucose** and other fuels into **ATP**, which powers **metabolism**. **Aerobic** respiration uses **oxygen** as the **final electron acceptor** and yields **far more ATP** than **glycolysis** alone.

---

**[Visual: Cytoplasm — glycolysis to pyruvate; small ATP and NADH labels]**

**Narrator:**  
**Glycolysis** happens in the **cytoplasm**: **glucose** splits to **two pyruvate**. There is a small **net** gain of **ATP** and **NADH** carries electrons toward the mitochondrion in **aerobic** cells.

---

**[Visual: Matrix — pyruvate to acetyl CoA; Krebs wheel spinning out CO₂ and reduced carriers]**

**Narrator:**  
Inside the **mitochondrial matrix**, **pyruvate** is converted to **acetyl CoA**, releasing **carbon dioxide**. The **Krebs cycle** completes **oxidation** of carbon to **CO₂**, generates more **reduced NAD and FAD**, and a little **substrate-level** ATP.

---

**[Visual: Cristae — ETC pumping protons; ATP synthase rotor]**

**Narrator:**  
On the **cristae**, electrons pass along the **electron transport chain**. Energy pumps **protons** into the **intermembrane space**. **Chemiosmosis** through **ATP synthase** makes **most** of the **ATP**. **Oxygen** accepts electrons at the end, forming **water**. Without oxygen, this stage **stops**.

---

**[Visual: Muscle — lactate; yeast — ethanol bubbles]**

**Narrator:**  
**Anaerobic** routes **regenerate NAD** so **glycolysis** keeps running. **Human muscle** reduces pyruvate to **lactate**. **Yeast** forms **ethanol** and **carbon dioxide**. Yields are **low**, but **fast** ATP helps **short bursts** or **fermentation** industries.

---

**[Visual: Respirometer — soda lime; manometer fluid level]**

**Narrator:**  
**Respiratory quotient** compares **carbon dioxide produced** to **oxygen used**. **Respirometer** questions need **temperature control**, **living** versus **dead** controls where appropriate, and for **plants** often **darkness** to stop **photosynthesis** masking **respiration**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — stages, sites, products, RQ, practical controls]**

**Narrator:**  
Know **where** each stage happens, **what** enters and leaves, and **why** **oxygen** matters for **aerobic** yield. Link **mitochondrial structure** to **function**. Next: connect to **photosynthesis** and **plant nutrition** for the full **carbon cycle** in cells.
