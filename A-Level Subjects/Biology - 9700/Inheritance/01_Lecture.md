## 16.1 Passage of information — Mendelian foundations

### Key terms

- **Gene:** length of DNA coding for a **polypeptide** / functional **RNA**.
- **Locus:** fixed position on a **chromosome**.
- **Allele:** **variant** of a gene at a locus.
- **Genotype:** alleles present; **phenotype:** **observable** characteristics (influenced by **environment** too).
- **Homozygous:** identical alleles; **heterozygous:** two different alleles.
- **Dominant** allele expressed in **heterozygote**; **recessive** only when **homozygous** (complete dominance model).

### Monohybrid inheritance

- **Punnett squares** predict offspring **genotype** ratios from **gamete** fusion.
- **Test cross:** cross **unknown dominant phenotype** with **homozygous recessive** to reveal **carrier** status.

### Dihybrid inheritance and independent assortment

- Two genes on **different** chromosomes (or **far apart**) → **9:3:3:1** phenotypic ratio (both **dominant** traits) from **dihybrid heterozygote** × **dihybrid heterozygote**.
- **Linkage:** genes on **same** chromosome tend to **inherit together** unless **crossing over** produces **recombinants**.

### Non-Mendelian patterns

- **Codominance** (e.g. **ABO blood** — **I^A**, **I^B**, **i**); **multiple alleles** at one locus.
- **Sex determination** (XY mammals): **X-linked** traits (e.g. **colour blindness**, **haemophilia**) show **different** male/female inheritance patterns.
- **Continuous variation** (height, yield): **polygenic** + **environment** → **normal distribution**; contrast with **discontinuous** (few distinct phenotypes).

### Mutation

- **Gene mutation:** **base** substitution / deletion / insertion → **different mRNA** / protein (e.g. **sickle-cell** **HbS**).
- **Chromosome mutation:** **non-disjunction**, **translocation**, **changes in number** (e.g. **Down’s syndrome** **trisomy 21**).

### Chi-squared test (goodness of fit)

- Compare **observed** offspring ratios to **expected** Mendelian ratios.
- **χ² = Σ (O − E)² / E**; compare to **critical value** at appropriate **degrees of freedom** (categories − 1) and **probability** (often **p = 0.05**).
- **Null hypothesis:** difference is **due to chance**; if **χ² < critical**, **do not reject** null.

## 16.2 Genes and phenotype

### From genotype to protein

- **Transcription** (nucleus) and **translation** (ribosomes) produce **primary structure**; **folding** and **modification** determine **functional** protein.
- **Enzyme** defects block **pathways** (e.g. **PKU**, **albinism** models in questions).

### Dominance at molecular level

- **Dominant negative** or **haploinsufficiency** extend beyond simple Mendel in advanced contexts—use **syllabus** examples only.

## 16.3 Gene control

### Prokaryotes — operon model (lac)

- **Structural genes** (e.g. **β-galactosidase**) **transcribed together** from **promoter**.
- **Repressor** binds **operator** → **blocked** transcription when **lactose** absent.
- **Lactose** (inducer) binds repressor → **RNA polymerase** accesses promoter → **ON**.

### Eukaryotes

- **Transcription factors** bind **enhancers/promoters**; **chromatin** remodelling (**acetylation** → **more accessible** DNA).
- **Alternative splicing** generates **multiple** mRNAs from one gene.
- **Epigenetic** awareness: **methylation** can **silence** genes without **DNA sequence** change (where syllabus includes).

## Genetic diagrams — exam technique

- **Clear P generation**, **gametes**, **F1**; **label** **male/female** where sex-linked.
- State **phenotype** beside **genotype**; use **standard allele** symbols.

## Common misconceptions

- **“Dominant”** does **not** mean “more common” in the population.
- **Female carriers** of **X-linked recessive** alleles are usually **phenotypically normal** but can pass **allele** to sons.
- **Expected ratios** apply to **large** sample sizes; **small families** deviate by **chance**.
