# Voice-Over Script: Inheritance (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Title — “9700 Topic 16: Inheritance” — DNA double helix + chromosomes]**

**Narrator:**  
**Inheritance** is how **alleles** pass from **parents** to **offspring**. Know **genotype** versus **phenotype**, **homozygous** versus **heterozygous**, and **dominant** versus **recessive** in the **complete dominance** model.

---

**[Visual: Punnett square — monohybrid cross; F1 ratios]**

**Narrator:**  
**Monohybrid crosses** predict **3:1** phenotypic ratios when both parents are **heterozygous** and one allele is **fully dominant**. A **test cross** with a **homozygous recessive** partner reveals whether a **dominant-looking** individual is **homozygous** or **heterozygous**.

---

**[Visual: Dihybrid 9:3:3:1 grid; second panel “linked genes — parental vs recombinant”]**

**Narrator:**  
**Dihybrid** inheritance gives **9:3:3:1** when genes **assort independently**. If genes are **linked** on the **same chromosome**, parental combinations are **more frequent** unless **crossing over** creates **recombinants**.

---

**[Visual: X and Y chromosomes — X-linked recessive pedigree]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sex linkage** often involves **recessive** alleles on the **X chromosome**. **Males** inherit **one X** from the mother—so **X-linked recessive** traits appear **more often** in males. **Females** can be **carriers**.

---

**[Visual: ABO blood alleles — codominance diagram]**

**Narrator:**  
**Codominance** and **multiple alleles** appear in systems like **ABO blood groups**, where **I A** and **I B** are **codominant** and both dominate **i**.

---

**[Visual: χ² table — observed vs expected row]**

**Narrator:**  
The **chi-squared test** checks whether **observed** offspring ratios **fit** **expected** Mendelian ratios. Calculate **chi-square**, read **degrees of freedom**, compare to **critical value**, and **accept or reject** the **null hypothesis** that **chance** alone explains the deviation.

---

**[Visual: Lac operon — repressor on/off with lactose]**

**Narrator:**  
**Gene control** in **bacteria** is illustrated by the **lac operon**: **lactose** **removes** a **repressor** from the **operator** so **RNA polymerase** can **transcribe** enzymes for **lactose** use. **Eukaryotes** use **transcription factors**, **chromatin** changes, and **splicing** for finer control.
