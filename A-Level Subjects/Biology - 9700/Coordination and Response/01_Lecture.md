## 15.1 Control and coordination in mammals

### Nervous vs hormonal systems

| Feature | **Nervous** | **Endocrine (hormonal)** |
|---------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Speed** | **Fast** (ms), short-lived | **Slower**, often **longer-lasting** |
| **Pathway** | **Neurones** + **synapses** | **Blood** transport to **target** cells with **receptors** |
| **Specificity** | **Exact** pathway | **Many** tissues may express receptors |

### Neurone structure and impulse transmission

- **Motor / sensory / relay** roles in **reflex arcs** and **CNS** pathways.
- **Myelinated** fibres: **saltatory** conduction at **nodes of Ranvier** → **faster** propagation.
- **Resting potential** (maintenance by **Na⁺/K⁺ pump** and selective permeability); **action potential**—**depolarisation** (voltage-gated **Na⁺** channels), **repolarisation** (**K⁺** efflux), **hyperpolarisation** then **recovery**.
- **All-or-nothing** nature; **frequency** codes **stimulus strength**.

### Synapses

- **Calcium** influx triggers **vesicle** fusion; **neurotransmitter** diffuses across **synaptic cleft**; binds **receptors** on **post-synaptic membrane** → **EPSP/IPSP**.
- **Unidirectional**; allows **summation**; **adaptation** and **drug** targets.

### Mammalian sense organ — the eye (core structure)

- **Cornea** / **aqueous humour** — **refraction**; **lens** — **fine** focusing (**accommodation** via **ciliary muscle** and **suspensory ligaments**).
- **Iris** controls **pupil** size (**light**); **retina** contains **rods** (**low light**, **monochrome**) and **cones** (**colour**, **acuity**).
- **Fovea** — dense **cones**; **optic nerve** to **brain**; **blind spot**.

### Hormonal examples (exam-heavy)

- **Adrenaline:** **fight or flight**—raises **heart rate**, **glycogen** breakdown, **bronchodilation**, **pupil** dilation (synergy with sympathetic nerves).
- **Blood glucose:** **insulin** (**β cells**) lowers glucose (**increased uptake**, **glycogenesis**); **glucagon** (**α cells**) raises glucose (**glycogenolysis**, **gluconeogenesis** in liver). **Negative feedback** stabilises **set point**.
- **ADH** (vasopressin): **collecting duct** **water permeability**; **osmoregulation** with **14.1** homeostasis.

## 15.2 Control and coordination in plants

### Plant growth regulators (“plant hormones”)

- **Auxins** (e.g. **IAA**): **cell elongation**; **phototropism** (**unequal** distribution → **differential growth**); used in **rooting** cuttings; **weedkillers** (synthetic auxins at high dose).
- **Gibberellins:** **stem elongation**, **seed germination**, **flowering** in some species.
- **Abscisic acid (ABA):** **stress** / **dormancy**; **stomatal** closure role.
- **Ethene (ethylene):** **fruit ripening**, **senescence**.

### Tropisms

- **Phototropism**—growth **toward** light; **gravitropism**—**roots** positive, **shoots** negative (statoliths / **amyloplasts** in **root cap** involved in perception in teaching models).

## Common misconceptions

- **Hormones** are **not** slower because they “lack nerves”—they diffuse/circulate and act via **receptor** binding kinetics.
- **Phototropism** is **growth** movement, **not** locomotion of the whole plant.
- **Insulin** and **glucagon** are **antagonistic**; both are **peptide/protein** hormones from **islets of Langerhans**.
