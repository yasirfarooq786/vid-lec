# Voice-Over Script: Coordination and Response (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Title — “9700 Topic 15: Control and coordination” — neurone + leaf tropism]**

**Narrator:**  
**Coordination** uses **electrical** signals in **nervous tissue** and **chemical** signals in **endocrine** systems. Nervous responses are **fast** and **pathway-specific**; **hormones** travel in **blood** to **target** cells with matching **receptors**.

---

**[Visual: Action potential graph — depolarisation spike along axon]**

**Narrator:**  
A **resting** neurone maintains a **potential difference** across its membrane. A **stimulus** triggers **voltage-gated** sodium channels; **sodium** rushes in and the membrane **depolarises**. **Potassium** channels restore **repolarisation**. In **myelinated** axons the impulse **jumps** between **nodes of Ranvier**—**saltatory** conduction is **faster**.

---

**[Visual: Synapse — vesicles, Ca²⁺, neurotransmitter, receptors]**

**Narrator:**  
At a **synapse**, **calcium** entry causes **synaptic vesicles** to fuse. **Neurotransmitter** crosses the **cleft** and binds **receptors**, generating a **post-synaptic** response. Transmission is **one-way** and allows **integration** of many inputs.

---

**[Visual: Eye diagram — lens rounding for near object]**

**Narrator:**  
The **eye** focuses light onto the **retina**. The **lens** changes shape for **accommodation**—**ciliary muscle** tension adjusts via **suspensory ligaments**. **Rods** work in **dim** light; **cones** give **colour** and **detail** at the **fovea**.

---

**[Visual: Pancreas islets — insulin down glucose, glucagon up]**

**Narrator:**  
**Insulin** from **beta cells** **lowers** blood glucose by increasing **cell uptake** and **storage** as **glycogen**. **Glucagon** from **alpha cells** **raises** glucose by **glycogen** breakdown in the **liver**. **Adrenaline** also **mobilises** glucose and prepares the body for **stress**—**faster heart**, wider **airways**, and more.

---

**[Visual: Seedling bending toward light — auxin concentration shading]**

**Narrator:**  
Plants use **growth regulators**. **Auxin** moves **away** from the **lit** side in shoots; **uneven** elongation causes **phototropism**. **Gibberellins** promote **stem** growth and **germination**; **abscisic acid** promotes **dormancy** and helps **close** stomata under stress; **ethene** speeds **ripening**.

Link **structure** to **function** in **neurones**, **synapses**, and **sense organs**. For plants, explain **tropisms** with **hormone** distribution. Cross-reference **homeostasis** for **glucose** and **water** balance questions.
