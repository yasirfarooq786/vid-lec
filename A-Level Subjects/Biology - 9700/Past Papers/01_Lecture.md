# Complete Lecture: Using Past Papers (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

This module is **not** syllabus biology content; it is a **structured approach** to **Cambridge 9700** examination papers so you gain **marks** from work you already understand. Official assessment overview: [Cambridge 9700](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-biology-9700/).

---

## Know your papers

| Paper | Focus | Typical skills |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Paper 1** | AS **multiple choice** | **Recall**, **application**, **data** in short stem |
| **Paper 2** | AS **structured** | **Definitions**, **diagrams**, **calculations**, **short explanations** |
| **Paper 3** | AS **Advanced Practical Skills** | **Manipulative** skills, **graphs**, **uncertainty** (context may be novel) |
| **Paper 4** | A Level **structured** | **Synoptic** AS + A2; **longer** reasoning |
| **Paper 5** | **Planning, analysis, evaluation** | **Hypotheses**, **variables**, **statistical** reasoning, **conclusions** |

Always confirm **timing** and **marks** on your **current** **specimen** papers—Cambridge **updates** schedules occasionally.

---

## Command words (read the stem twice)

- **State / name / give:** short, **accurate** fact—**no** long essay.
- **Describe:** **observations** or **sequence**—**what** happens; **figures** from graphs **with units**.
- **Explain:** **because** / **therefore**—**mechanism** or **reason** linking **biology** to the **outcome**.
- **Compare:** **differences** **and** **similarities** (use **both** where asked).
- **Suggest:** **reasonable** hypothesis from **data**—**not** wild guess; **justify** from **evidence**.
- **Evaluate / discuss:** **balance** (benefits vs limitations), **evidence**, **uncertainty**.

**Underlining** key words in the question helps you **match** the **mark scheme** style.

---

## Mark schemes are the hidden textbook

- Note **acceptable** **alternative** phrasing and **condoned** errors.
- See **how many** **marks** → **how many** **distinct points** (often **one mark per clear idea**).
- Learn **exact** **syllabus** **terms** mark schemes **reward** (e.g. **“hydrogen bonds”**, **“partially permeable”**, **“gene”** vs loose wording).

---

## Data-handling routine

1. **Read** **axes**—**units**, **linear** vs **log** scale.
2. **Quote** **trends** with **numbers** (e.g. “from **12** to **36 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹** between **20 °C** and **30 °C**”).
3. **Relate** to **biology** (enzyme kinetic, limiting factor, membrane permeability).
4. **Uncertainty:** error bars, **overlap** → **no significant** difference (if question uses that framework).

---

## Practical and planning papers (3 and 5)

- **Independent / dependent / controlled** variables—**table** with **units**.
- **Fair test:** **one** factor changed; **others** constant; **replicates** and **mean**.
- **Risk** sensible for **stated** chemicals/apparatus.
- **Evaluation:** **systematic** vs **random** error, **resolution** of instruments, **improvements** that **directly** address **weakness**.

---

## Revision cycle (one topic)

1. **Revise** from your **notes** / **lecture** (e.g. this folder).
2. **Closed book** — attempt **real** past question **without** notes.
3. **Mark** with **scheme**; **highlight** **missed** **keywords**.
4. **Rewrite** **only** **failed** parts in **mark-scheme** language.
5. **Reattempt** a **similar** question **one week** later.

---

## Time management

- **Scan** whole paper; **allocate** time **by marks** (e.g. **1 min per mark** as a rough guide, faster on MCQ).
- **Long** **9-mark** items: **plan** **three** **paragraphs** (intro mechanism → evidence → conclusion) **before** writing.

---

## Integrity and effective use

- Past papers are for **learning** **exam technique**; **centre** **integrity** rules apply to **assessed** work. Use **official** Cambridge material or **licensed** sources your teacher provides.
