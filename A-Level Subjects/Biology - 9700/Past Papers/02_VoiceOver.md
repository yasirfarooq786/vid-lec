# Voice-Over Script: Using Past Papers (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Title — “Past papers: exam technique for 9700”]**

**Narrator:**  
Past papers are most useful when you treat them as **training**, not trivia. Know **which paper** tests **which skills**: **Paper 1** multiple choice, **Paper 2** AS structured, **Paper 3** practical, **Paper 4** full A Level structured, **Paper 5** planning and **data evaluation**.

---

**[Visual: Highlighted command words — State, Explain, Compare]**

**Narrator:**  
Read **command words** literally. **State** wants a **concise** fact. **Explain** needs a **mechanism**—**link** cause to effect. **Compare** must include **both** sides, not only differences. **Suggest** should follow **logically** from the **data** in the stem.

---

**[Visual: Mark scheme excerpt — ticks per marking point]**

**Narrator:**  
After each attempt, **mark yourself** with the **official mark scheme**. Count how many **separate ideas** earn a mark, then **rewrite** **only** what you missed using **their** **acceptable** **phrasing**. That is how **vocabulary** becomes automatic.

---

**[Visual: Graph with cursor reading values — units circled]**

**Narrator:**  
For **data** questions, always quote **values** with **units**, describe **trends** across the **x-axis**, and **link** the pattern to **biology**—for example **limiting factors**, **enzyme** denaturation, or **membrane** behaviour.

---

**[Visual: Table — independent, dependent, control variables]**

**Narrator:**  
Papers **3** and **5** reward **clear variables**, **controls**, **replicates**, and **honest evaluation** of **errors**. Propose **improvements** that **directly** fix a **stated** weakness—bigger sample, **finer** resolution, **buffer** to stabilise pH, and so on.

---

**[Visual: Timer — marks to minutes]**

**Narrator:**  
Practise **under time pressure**. Divide **total minutes** by **marks** to set a **pace**. For **long** answers, spend **one minute** planning **bullet** points so your **first** sentence already **answers** the **question**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — timed paper, self-mark, rewrite weak points]**

**Narrator:**  
Your loop is **attempt**, **mark**, **fix**, **repeat**. One **deep** paper session beats **passive** rereading. Now schedule a **full** **Paper 2** or **Paper 4** section **without** notes.
