# Voice-Over Script: Ecology (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Title — “Ecology & Topic 18: Biodiversity” — globe with habitats]**

**Narrator:**  
**Ecology** in **9700** ties **classification**, **biodiversity**, **conservation**, and **ecosystem** processes. Classification gives **stable names** and reflects **evolutionary** relationships using **shared** characters.

---

**[Visual: Binomial name on specimen label — Genus species]**

**Narrator:**  
Every species has a **two-part Latinised** name: **genus** and **species** epithet. **Dichotomous keys** step through **paired** choices to **identify** unknown specimens.

---

**[Visual: Three panels — genetic variants, many species forest, different habitats]**

**Narrator:**  
**Biodiversity** exists at **genetic**, **species**, and **ecosystem** levels. High diversity can support **stability** and provides **resources** and **services** such as **pollination**, **clean water**, and **carbon storage**.

---

**[Visual: National park vs zoo and seed bank — split screen]**

**Narrator:**  
**Conservation** uses **in situ** protection—**habitats** and **corridors**—and **ex situ** methods like **zoos**, **captive breeding**, and **seed banks**. Each approach has **strengths**: wild protection preserves **whole communities**; ex situ can **rescue** species on the **brink** but risks **low genetic diversity**.

---

**[Visual: Food web — arrows to consumers; pyramid of energy]**

**Narrator:**  
**Energy** flows **one way**: **producers** capture light; **herbivores** pass only a **small fraction** to the next level—often roughly **ten percent** as a teaching rule—so **pyramids** of **energy** are **upright**. **Nutrients** like **carbon** and **nitrogen** **cycle** through **living** and **non-living** stores, driven by **decomposers** and **microbes**.

---

**[Visual: Logistic curve — carrying capacity K]**

**Narrator:**  
**Populations** grow **exponentially** when limits are absent, then approach **carrying capacity** as **limiting factors** bite. **Succession** rebuilds communities after disturbance, from **pioneers** toward a **climax** system with changing **species composition** and **diversity**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — classification, threats, conservation, energy vs nutrients]**

**Narrator:**  
Revise **definitions**, **examples**, and **synoptic** links to **human activity**—**deforestation**, **pollution**, **climate change**—and **management** responses. Connect to **evolution** for **why** species differ and **why** preserving **genetic** diversity matters.
