## 18.1 Classification

### Why classify?

- **Identify** organisms, **communicate** unambiguously, **infer** relationships and **evolutionary** history.

### Binomial nomenclature

- **Genus** + **species** epithet (italic print or underlined); **Latinised** forms (e.g. *Homo sapiens*).

### Three domains and five kingdoms (teaching overview)

- **Domains:** **Bacteria**, **Archaea**, **Eukarya** (differences in **rRNA**, **membrane lipids**, **histones**, etc., at extension level).
- **Kingdoms** (traditional teaching): **Prokaryotae**, **Protoctista**, **Fungi**, **Plantae**, **Animalia**—use **features** your specification lists (cell structure, nutrition, body plan).

### Dichotomous keys

- Paired **couplets** of contrasting characters lead to **identification**; **reliable** if characters are **clear** and **stable**.

## 18.2 Biodiversity

### Levels

- **Genetic diversity** within species; **species richness** and **evenness**; **ecosystem / habitat** diversity.

### Measuring diversity (where examined)

- **Species count** in samples; **Simpson’s index of diversity** or **Shannon** (learn **formula** and **interpretation** if your course requires—**higher** value → **more diverse** in Simpson’s **D** context depending on variant—**check definition used**).

### Importance

- **Stability**, **resilience**, **resources** (medicines, crops), **aesthetic/cultural** value, **ecosystem services**.

---

## 18.3 Conservation

### Threats

- **Habitat loss**, **overexploitation**, **invasive species**, **pollution**, **climate change**.

### Strategies

- **In situ:** **protected areas**, **national parks**, **wildlife corridors**, **legislation**.
- **Ex situ:** **captive breeding**, **botanic gardens**, **seed banks**, **zoos** (genetic **bottleneck** and **reintroduction** issues).
- **CITES**, **IUCN Red List** awareness; **sustainable** use (quotas, fishing nets, forestry).

## Ecosystem structure and energy (synoptic content)

### Trophic levels

- **Producers** (photosynthetic / chemosynthetic) → **primary consumers** → **secondary** → **tertiary**; **decomposers** / **saprotrophs** recycle **nutrients**.

### Food chains and webs

- **Arrows** show **energy flow** (to **consumer**); webs show **stability** through **alternative** pathways.

### Productivity and pyramids

- **Gross** vs **net primary production**; **efficiency** of transfer between levels often roughly **~10%** (wide variation)—explains **pyramid of numbers**, **biomass**, or **energy** (**inverted** pyramids possible for **numbers**—e.g. parasites on one tree).

### Nutrient cycles (outline)

- **Carbon:** **photosynthesis** removes **CO₂**; **respiration**, **decomposition**, **combustion** return it.
- **Nitrogen:** **nitrogen fixation**, **nitrification**, **denitrification**, **ammonification**; **nitrate** uptake by plants; **legume** **root nodules** with **Rhizobium**.

### Population ecology

- **Exponential** growth when **unlimited**; **logistic** growth with **carrying capacity (K)**; **limiting factors** (food, predation, disease, space).

### Succession

- **Primary** (bare rock) vs **secondary** (after disturbance); **pioneer** species → **climax community**; **biodiversity** often rises then may **stabilise**.

## Human impacts and management

- **Deforestation** → **CO₂**, **soil erosion**, **habitat loss**.
- **Agriculture:** **monoculture** vs **polyculture**; **pesticides** and **non-target** effects; **eutrophication** from **fertiliser** run-off.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **“Biodiversity”** is **not** only **species count**—includes **genetic** and **ecosystem** levels.
- **Energy** is **not** recycled in ecosystems; **nutrients** are.
- **Conservation** is **not** only **zoos**—**in situ** protection of **habitats** is often **preferred**.
