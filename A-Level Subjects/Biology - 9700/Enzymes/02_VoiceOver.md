# Voice-Over Script: Enzymes (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Title — “9700 Topic 3: Enzymes” — enzyme + substrate → products animation]**

**Narrator:**  
Topic **3** is about **biological catalysts**. Enzymes are usually **globular proteins** with a specific **active site**. They **speed up** reactions by **lowering activation energy** and are **not used up** in the net equation.

---

**[Visual: Lock-and-key morphing to induced fit — bond strain in substrate]**

**Narrator:**  
The **substrate** binds forming an **enzyme–substrate complex**. The **induced fit** idea explains a slight **shape change** that improves binding and helps **break or make bonds**. Products **leave**; the enzyme is **free** again.

---

**[Visual: Temperature graph — rise to peak then sharp fall; label “denaturation”]**

**Narrator:**  
**Temperature** affects **collision rate** and **enzyme shape**. Warming increases **kinetic energy** and **rate** up to an **optimum**. Too hot **denatures** the enzyme—**bonds** maintaining the **tertiary structure** break and the **active site** is lost, so **rate collapses**.

---

**[Visual: pH scale with pepsin vs trypsin optimum markers]**

**Narrator:**  
**pH** changes **charges** on amino acid side chains, so each enzyme has an **optimum pH**. **Buffers** are essential in practical work so pH does not **drift** during the reaction.

---

**[Visual: Hyperbolic curve — initial rate vs substrate concentration; “saturation / Vmax”]**

**Narrator:**  
As **substrate concentration** rises, **initial rate** increases until **every active site** is busy—that is **saturation** and **maximum rate** for that enzyme amount. **More enzyme** shifts the ceiling upward if substrate stays in excess.

---

**[Visual: Competitive inhibitor blocked active site vs non-competitive shape change]**

**Narrator:**  
**Competitive inhibitors** compete for the **active site**; **high substrate** can **outcompete** them. **Non-competitive inhibitors** bind **elsewhere**, **distort** the enzyme, and **lower** the maximum rate. **End-product inhibition** is a classic **feedback** control on pathways.

---

**[Visual: Alginate beads in column bioreactor]**

**Narrator:**  
**Immobilised enzymes** in beads or on supports are used industrially so enzymes can be **reused** and separated from **product** easily.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — initial rate, controls, repeats, safety]**

**Narrator:**  
For exams, always discuss **initial rate**, **controlled variables**, and **fair comparison**. Practise graph sketches and **explain** every **curve** in terms of **collisions** and **active site shape**. Next is **Topic 4: Cell membranes and transport**.
