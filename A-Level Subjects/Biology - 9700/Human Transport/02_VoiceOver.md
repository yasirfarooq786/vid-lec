# Voice-Over Script: Human Transport (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Double circulation diagram — pulmonary and systemic loops]**

**Narrator:**  
Topic **8** is **transport in mammals**. Humans have a **closed double circulation**: one circuit through the **lungs**, one through the **body**. The **left ventricle** drives the **high-pressure systemic** side; the **right ventricle** pumps **deoxygenated** blood to the **lungs**.

---

**[Visual: Cross-sections — thick elastic artery, valve in vein, single-file RBCs in capillary]**

**Narrator:**  
**Arteries** carry blood **from the heart**. Walls are **thick**, **elastic**, and **muscular**—they **recoil** between heartbeats. **Veins** return blood at **lower pressure**; **valves** prevent backflow. **Capillaries** are **one cell thick** for **exchange**—oxygen, nutrients, and wastes move between **blood** and **tissue fluid**.

---

**[Visual: Haemoglobin quaternary structure — O₂ loading in lung, unloading in muscle]**

**Narrator:**  
**Red blood cells** are packed with **haemoglobin**. Each molecule binds **up to four oxygen** molecules in the **lungs**, forming **oxyhaemoglobin**. The **dissociation curve** is **S-shaped** because binding is **cooperative**. In **active tissues**, **lower pH** and **higher carbon dioxide** shift the curve **right**—the **Bohr effect**—so **more oxygen** is **released** where it is needed.

---

**[Visual: CO₂ pathways — dissolved, carbamino, hydrogencarbonate; RBC with carbonic anhydrase]**

**Narrator:**  
**Carbon dioxide** travels **dissolved**, as **carbamino** compounds on **haemoglobin**, and mainly as **hydrogencarbonate** after **carbonic anhydrase** speeds reaction with water. **Chloride ions** move into red cells to balance charge—the **chloride shift**. **Haemoglobin** also **buffers** hydrogen ions from that reaction.

---

**[Visual: Heart chambers — valves opening/closing; SAN/AVN conduction animation]**

**Narrator:**  
The **heart** has **four chambers** and **four valves**. **Ventricular systole** closes **AV valves**—the **first heart sound**—then opens **semilunar** valves to eject blood. **Diastole** refills the ventricles. Electrical activity starts at the **SAN**, delays at the **AVN**, then spreads through the **bundle of His** and **Purkinje fibres** for a **coordinated** ventricular contraction.
