## 8.1 The circulatory system

### Open versus closed; single versus double

- **Closed circulation:** blood confined in **vessels** (vertebrates).
- **Double circulation:** blood passes through the **heart twice** per complete circuit—**pulmonary** (heart → lungs → heart) and **systemic** (heart → body → heart). **Higher pressure** to tissues; **oxygenated** and **deoxygenated** blood kept largely separate in birds and mammals.

### Major blood vessels (overview)

- **Aorta** (systemic artery from left ventricle); **vena cava** (to right atrium).
- **Pulmonary artery** (right ventricle → lungs—**deoxygenated** in mammals); **pulmonary veins** (lungs → left atrium—**oxygenated**).
- **Coronary arteries** supply **cardiac muscle**; blockage → **myocardial infarction**.
- **Hepatic portal vein** carries blood from **digestive organs** to **liver** for **processing** absorbed nutrients and **detoxification** before blood returns to general circulation.

### Structure and function of arteries, veins, capillaries

| Vessel | Wall thickness / muscle / elastic | Lumen | Function |
|--------|-------------------------------------|-------|----------|
| **Artery** | **Thick**, **elastic** + **smooth muscle** | Relatively **narrow** (except near heart) | Carry blood **away from heart** at **high pressure**; **elastic recoil** smooths pressure (**Windkessel** effect) |
| **Vein** | **Thinner** wall, **less elastic** | **Wide** lumen, often **valves** | **Low pressure** return; **skeletal muscle** + **respiratory** movements assist flow |
| **Capillary** | **Single endothelial layer** (fenestrated in some organs) | **Very narrow**—**RBCs** pass **single file** | **Exchange** by diffusion, facilitated diffusion, and (for some fluids) **bulk flow** via **pores/slits** |

### Blood components

- **Plasma:** water, **plasma proteins** (e.g. **albumin** for **oncotic pressure**), **nutrients**, **hormones**, **CO₂** (as **hydrogencarbonate**), **waste**.
- **Red blood cells (erythrocytes):** **biconcave** → large **surface area : volume**; **no nucleus** in mature human RBCs; packed with **haemoglobin**.
- **White blood cells:** **immune** roles (phagocytes, lymphocytes—developed in immunity topic).
- **Platelets:** **blood clotting**.

### Tissue fluid and lymph (where examined)

- At **arteriolar** end of capillary beds, **hydrostatic pressure** forces **plasma** (minus large proteins) through gaps → **tissue fluid** bathes cells.
- **Water** and **solutes** return at **venous** end by **oncotic** gradient; excess drains to **lymphatic** system → **lymph** → **subclavian veins**.

## 8.2 Transport of oxygen and carbon dioxide

### Haemoglobin and oxygen

- **Haemoglobin (Hb):** quaternary protein with **four polypeptide chains**, each with a **haem** group containing **Fe²⁺** that binds **one O₂** molecule.
- **Loading** in **lungs** (high **pO₂**) → **oxyhaemoglobin**; **unloading** in **respiring tissues** (lower **pO₂**).

### Oxygen dissociation curve

- **Sigmoid (S-shaped)** curve: **cooperative** binding—first O₂ binding **increases** affinity for further O₂ (**positive cooperativity**).
- **Steep** middle region: small change in **pO₂** causes **large** change in **saturation**—efficient **loading/unloading** in working range.

### Bohr effect (Bohr shift)

- **Lower pH** (higher **H⁺**, e.g. in **actively respiring** muscle) **shifts curve** **to the right** → **lower affinity** for O₂ → **more O₂ released** to tissues.
- **Higher pCO₂** contributes via **carbonic acid** formation.

### Carbon dioxide transport

1. **Dissolved** in plasma (~**7%** in typical teaching proportions).
2. **Carbamino compounds** (~**23%**): CO₂ binds **amino groups** on **haemoglobin** (**carbaminohaemoglobin**).
3. **Hydrogencarbonate** in plasma (~**70%**): **CO₂ + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻** catalysed by **carbonic anhydrase** (RBCs). **HCO₃⁻** diffuses to plasma; **chloride shift** (**Cl⁻** into RBC) maintains **electrical balance**.
- **Haemoglobin** buffers **H⁺** (**haemoglobinic acid**) limiting **pH** change—**essential** for **Bohr** unloading and **CO₂** carriage.

### Carbon monoxide (where syllabus includes)

- **Competes** for haem binding with **much higher affinity** than O₂ → **severe hypoxia** even if pO₂ seems adequate.

## 8.3 The heart

### External and internal structure

- **Four chambers:** **right atrium**, **right ventricle**, **left atrium**, **left ventricle**.
- **Left ventricle** wall **thickest**—pumps **systemic** circulation at **highest pressure**.
- **Valves:** **tricuspid** (right AV), **bicuspid / mitral** (left AV), **pulmonary** and **aortic** **semilunar** valves—prevent **backflow**.
- **Septum** separates **oxygenated** and **deoxygenated** sides.

### Cardiac cycle (simplified)

- **Atrial systole:** atria contract → blood through **AV valves** into ventricles.
- **Ventricular systole:** **pressure** closes AV valves (**first heart sound**); ventricles contract → opens **semilunar** valves → ejection to **pulmonary artery** / **aorta**.
- **Diastole:** ventricles **relax**; semilunar valves **close** (**second heart sound**); **venous filling** of atria; ventricles fill (**passive** then **atrial systole** next cycle).

### Conducting system

- **SAN** (**sinoatrial node**) — **pacemaker**, intrinsic rhythm; wave spreads across **atria**.
- **AVN** (**atrioventricular node**) — delay allows **atrial emptying**.
- **Bundle of His** and **Purkinje fibres** — rapid spread through **ventricular** walls for **coordinated** contraction from **apex** upward.

### Heart rate control (brief)

- **Medulla** via **sympathetic** (accelerator) and **parasympathetic vagus** (brake) nerves; **adrenaline** increases rate and stroke volume.

## Common misconceptions

- **Pulmonary artery** carries **deoxygenated** blood—it is still an **artery** (away from heart).
- **Tissue fluid** is **not** inside cells; **cytoplasm** is intracellular.
- **Left ventricle** does **not** pump blood to the **lungs**.
