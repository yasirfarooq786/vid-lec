## Part A — Transport in plants (Topic 7 overview)

### Transport tissues

- **Xylem:** **vessels** (dead at maturity, **lignified** walls, **no cytoplasm**), **tracheids** in some plants; **apoplast** pathway; **cohesion–tension** theory for **ascent of water**.
- **Phloem:** **sieve tube elements** (living, **few organelles**, **sieve plates**) with **companion cells** (**load** assimilates, provide **ATP** and **proteins**); **mass flow** (pressure-flow) model for **translocation**.

### Water uptake and movement

- **Root hair cells** → **increased surface area**; **active transport** of mineral ions can **lower water potential** in **cytoplasm/vacuole** → **osmosis** of water from soil.
- **Casparian strip** (suberin in **endodermal** cell walls) forces water and ions through **symplast** / **selective** pathways into **xylem**—**regulates** ion entry.
- **Transpiration pull:** **evaporation** from mesophyll → **tension** in **continuous water column** (**cohesion** between water molecules, **adhesion** to xylem walls); **stomata** regulate **water loss** vs **gas exchange**.

### Translocation of assimilates

- **Source** (e.g. **photosynthetic leaf**) loads **sucrose** into phloem (often **companion cell / sieve tube** **co-transport** with **protons**).
- **Sink** (root, meristem, fruit) **unloads** sucrose.
- **Mass flow:** **hydrostatic pressure** gradient from **source** to **sink** (high pressure at source, lower at sink) drives **bulk flow** in **sieve tubes**.

## Part B — Photosynthesis (Topic 13)

### Overview equation (simplified)

**Carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen** (light energy captured by chlorophyll).

Occurs in **chloroplasts**: **thylakoids** (grana) and **stroma**.

### Light-dependent reactions

- **Photosystems** I and II absorb light; **chlorophyll** and **accessory pigments** funnel energy to **reaction centres**.
- **Photolysis** of water in PSII provides **electrons**, **protons**, and **oxygen** (waste product released).
- **Electron transport chain** generates **ATP** (**photophosphorylation**) and reduces **NADP** to **NADPH**.
- **Non-cyclic** photophosphorylation: flow from **PSII → PSI**; **cyclic** (PSI only) yields extra **ATP** without **NADPH** or O₂ (context-dependent).

### Light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle)

- In **stroma**; **RuBisCO** fixes **CO₂** to **RuBP** (5C) → unstable 6C splits to **two GP** (3C).
- **ATP** and **NADPH** reduce **GP** to **TP** (triose phosphate).
- **Regeneration** of **RuBP** consumes most TP; some TP exits to make **hexose sugars**, **amino acids**, **lipids**, etc.

### Limiting factors (Topic 13.2)

- **Light intensity**, **carbon dioxide concentration**, **temperature** (enzyme-limited stages).
- At any moment, the **slowest factor** **limits** the overall rate (**law of limiting factors**).
- **Graphs:** e.g. **CO₂** vs rate at **two** light levels—higher light raises plateau until **another** factor limits.

### Leaf adaptations (gas exchange + light capture)

- **Palisade mesophyll**—**chloroplast-rich**, **columnar**, upper surface for **light**.
- **Spongy mesophyll**—**air spaces** for **diffusion** of **CO₂** and **O₂**.
- **Stomata** (guard cells) regulate **gas exchange** and **transpiration**; **cuticle** reduces water loss; **xylem** and **phloem** in **vascular bundles**.

## Mineral ions (exam essentials)

| Ion / source | Role |
|--------------|------|
| **Nitrate** | **Amino acids**, proteins, nucleotides, chlorophyll |
| **Magnesium** | **Chlorophyll** centre |
| **Phosphate** | **ATP**, **DNA/RNA**, membrane phospholipids |
| **Potassium** | **Stomatal** opening (guard cell turgor), enzyme cofactor |
| **Calcium** | **Cell wall** / membrane stability (cross-links pectins) |

**Deficiency symptoms** often tested: **chlorosis** (Mg, N), **poor growth**, etc.

## Practical and data skills

- **Investigate limiting factors** (e.g. **pondweed**, **bubbles/minute** or **oxygen sensor**)—control **one** factor, **repeat**, **full range** of chosen variable.
- **Transpiration** potometers—**environmental** variables (wind, humidity, temperature, light).

## Common misconceptions

- **Photosynthesis** does **not** “complete in the leaf only”—**products** are **exported** to sinks.
- **Xylem** transports **water and mineral ions** **up** mainly; **phloem** moves **assimilates** **bidirectionally** source → sink.
- **Oxygen** from **photolysis** is **not** the same O₂ used in **mitochondrial** respiration in the same simplified diagram—track **which process** releases O₂.
