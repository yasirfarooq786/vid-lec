# Voice-Over Script: Plant Nutrition (Cambridge International AS & A Level Biology 9700)

**[Visual: Plant cross-section — root hair, xylem/phloem in vascular bundle, leaf]**

**Narrator:**  
**Plant nutrition** in **9700** links **water and mineral uptake**, **transport**, and **photosynthesis**. **Xylem** moves **water and ions** mainly **up** the plant. **Phloem** moves **sucrose** and other assimilates from **sources** like leaves to **sinks** like roots and fruits.

---

**[Visual: Cohesion–tension — water column in xylem; stomata open/close]**

**Narrator:**  
Water enters by **osmosis** at **root hairs**, often driven by **active ion uptake** lowering **water potential**. The **Casparian strip** in the **endodermis** forces selective entry into the **xylem**. In the shoot, **transpiration** from leaves creates **tension**; **cohesion** between water molecules and **adhesion** to xylem walls supports the **cohesion–tension** model. **Stomata** balance **CO₂ gain** with **water loss**.

---

**[Visual: Sieve tube + companion cell — arrow “loading sucrose at source”]**

**Narrator:**  
**Phloem translocation** uses **mass flow**. **Companion cells** use **ATP** to **load** sucrose at the **source**, raising **sucrose concentration** and **osmotic** water entry → **high pressure**. At the **sink**, sucrose is **removed**, pressure falls, and sap **flows** along **sieve tubes**.

---

**[Visual: Chloroplast — grana and stroma labelled]**

**Narrator:**  
**Photosynthesis** splits into **light-dependent** reactions on **thylakoid membranes** and the **Calvin cycle** in the **stroma**. Light drives **ATP** and **NADPH** and splits **water** to yield **oxygen**. **RuBisCO** fixes **carbon dioxide** onto **RuBP**; **triose phosphate** is reduced using that **ATP** and **NADPH**, and **RuBP** is **regenerated**.

---

**[Visual: Three graphs — light, CO₂, temperature; “limiting factor” label]**

**Narrator:**  
**Rate** is limited by whichever factor is **least favourable**—**light**, **carbon dioxide**, or **temperature** affecting enzymes. Sketch **two** **CO₂** curves at **different** light intensities: the **higher** light curve **plateaus higher** until something else limits.

---

**[Visual: Leaf — palisade vs spongy; stomata guard cells]**

**Narrator:**  
**Palisade** cells pack **chloroplasts** for **light capture**; **spongy** tissue has **air spaces** for **gas diffusion**. **Magnesium** is in **chlorophyll**; **nitrate** builds **proteins** and **nucleic acids**. Link **deficiency symptoms** to **roles**.
