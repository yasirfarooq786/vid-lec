# Complete Lecture: Trigonometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**Trigonometry** links **angles** to **ratios** in **triangles** and **periodic functions** on **ℝ**. Central to **Pure 1–3** and **Mechanics**. Programme: [Cambridge 9709](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-mathematics-9709/).

---

## 1. Right-angled triangle

**sin θ = opp/hyp**, **cos θ = adj/hyp**, **tan θ = opp/adj = sin θ/cos θ**

---

## 2. Sine and cosine rules (any triangle)

**a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C** (**sine rule**)

**a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A** (**cosine rule**)

**Area = ½ bc sin A**

---

## 3. Pythagorean identity

**sin²θ + cos²θ ≡ 1**

**Dividing:** **1 + tan²θ ≡ sec²θ**; **1 + cot²θ ≡ cosec²θ**

---

## 4. Solving equations

- **Use identities** to **reduce** to **single** trig function.  
- **Factorise** e.g. **2sin²x − sin x = 0** → **sin x(2sin x − 1) = 0**.  
- **General solution** or **specified interval** — list **all** solutions in **range** (CAST / **graph** sketch).

**sin θ = k** → **θ = arcsin k + 2nπ** or **π − arcsin k + 2nπ** (adjust for **domain**).

---

## 5. Compound-angle formulae

**sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B**  
**cos(A ± B) = cos A cos B ∓ sin A sin B**  
**tan(A ± B) = (tan A ± tan B)/(1 ∓ tan A tan B)**

**Double angle:** **sin 2A = 2 sin A cos A**; **cos 2A = cos²A − sin²A = 2cos²A − 1 = 1 − 2sin²A**

---

## 6. R–α form (a cos θ + b sin θ)

**R cos(θ − α)** or **R sin(θ + α)** with **R = √(a² + b²)**, **tan α = b/a** (quadrant from **signs** of **a**, **b**).

**Use:** **max/min**, **harmonic** motion, **solving** **linear** **combination** equations.

---

## 7. Small angles (radians)

**sin θ ≈ θ**, **tan θ ≈ θ**, **cos θ ≈ 1 − θ²/2** for **small |θ|**

---

## 8. Graphs

**y = sin x**, **cos x**, **tan x**: **period** **2π** (sin, cos) or **π** (tan); **asymptotes** of **tan** at **odd multiples** of **π/2**.

**Transformations:** **amplitude**, **period** change (**sin kx** has **period 2π/k**), **phase shift**.

---

## Common mistakes

- **Calculator** in **wrong** **mode** (**deg** vs **rad**).  
- **Losing** solutions when **squaring** both sides.  
- **Wrong** **quadrant** for **α** in **R-form**.
