# Voice-Over Script: Trigonometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Triangle — SOH CAH TOA labels]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sine**, **cosine**, and **tangent** encode **ratios** in **right** triangles. For **any** triangle, **sine rule** and **cosine rule** generalise **Pythagoras** and **SOHCAHTOA**.

---

**[Visual: Unit circle — sin and cos as coordinates]**

**Narrator:**  
On the **unit circle**, **cos theta** and **sin theta** are **x** and **y**. That picture finds **all** **solutions** to **trig** equations—use **symmetry** instead of **memorising** endless **formulas**.

---

**[Visual: Identity sin²+cos²=1 morphing into tan identity]**

**Narrator:**  
**Pythagorean** identity is the **master** relation. **Divide** by **cos squared** to get **one plus tan squared equals sec squared**. Most **proof** questions **factor** or **substitute** back to **this**.

---

**[Visual: sin(A+B) expansion with rotating triangles]**

**Narrator:**  
**Compound angle** formulas expand **sin of A plus B** into **products** of **sines** and **cosines**. They feed **double angle** formulas and let you **solve** **equations** like **sin x plus cos x equals one** by **combining** into a **single** **sine wave**.

---

**[Visual: R cos(θ−α) — amplitude R, phase shift α]**

**Narrator:**  
**R alpha form** writes **a cos theta plus b sin theta** as one **cosine** curve with **amplitude** root **a squared plus b squared** and **phase** **alpha** fixed by **tan alpha equals b over a**—watch the **quadrant**.

---

**Narrator:**  
Set your **calculator** to **radians** for **calculus** questions. **Check** you have **every** solution in the **given** **range**. Next: **vectors**.
