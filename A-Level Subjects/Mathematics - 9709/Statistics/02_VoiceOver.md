# Voice-Over Script: Statistics (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Histogram — frequency density bars]**

**Narrator:**  
In a **histogram**, **area** represents **frequency**, so the **vertical** axis is often **frequency density**—**frequency** divided by **class width**. **Unequal** **widths** **force** you to **use** **density**, not **raw** **height**.

---

**[Visual: Mean dot on number line; spread arrows for sd]**

**Narrator:**  
The **mean** is the **balance point** of the **data**. **Standard deviation** measures **typical** **distance** from the **mean**. For **grouped** data, assume **everyone** sits at the **midpoint**—state that **assumption** if asked.

---

**[Visual: Scatter plot — regression line through cloud]**

**Narrator:**  
**Correlation r** measures **linear** **trend** between **minus one** and **plus one**. The **regression line of y on x** **predicts** **y** from **x** by **minimising** **vertical** **errors**—don’t flip **roles** unless the **question** asks for **x on y**.

---

**[Visual: Normal curve — shaded tail P(Z < z)]**

**Narrator:**  
**Standardise** any **normal** variable by **subtracting mu** and **dividing by sigma**. Then use **tables** or **calculator** for **probabilities**. **Sketch** the **bell** to see **tails** and **avoid** **one minus** mistakes.

---

**[Visual: Stratified sampling cartoon]**

**Narrator:**  
**Good sampling** **reduces bias**. **Stratified** sampling **fixes** **proportions** from **subgroups** so **minorities** aren’t **accidentally** **missed**.

---
