# Complete Lecture: Statistics (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**Statistics** summarises **data**, quantifies **spread**, and introduces **inference** with **distributions** and **sampling**. Programme: [Cambridge 9709](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-mathematics-9709/).

---

## 1. Data presentation

**Histogram:** **area** ∝ **frequency** (frequency density = freq / class width).  
**Cumulative frequency** → **median**, **quartiles** by **interpolation**.  
**Box-and-whisker:** **five-number summary**, **outliers** (rule per **question**).

---

## 2. Measures of location

**Mean** **x̄ = Σx/n** or **Σxf/Σf** (grouped — **midpoint** assumption).  
**Median**, **mode**.

---

## 3. Measures of spread

**Range** = max − min  
**Interquartile range** **IQR = Q₃ − Q₁**  
**Variance** **s² = Σ(x − x̄)²/n** or **/(n−1)** for **sample** (follow **formula booklet**).  
**Standard deviation** **s = √variance**

**Linear transform** **y = ax + b:** **Var(y) = a² Var(x)**

---

## 4. Linear correlation and regression

**Scatter diagram** — **positive/negative** association.

**Product-moment correlation coefficient r** (−1 ≤ r ≤ 1): **strength** and **direction** of **linear** association (formula from **booklet**).

**Regression line y on x:** **least squares** — **predict y** from **x** (not **symmetric** with **x on y**).

**Interpret** **gradient** and **intercept** in **context**.

---

## 5. Normal distribution **N(μ, σ²)**

**Bell curve**; **standardise:** **Z = (X − μ)/σ**

**Use tables** / **calculator** for **P(X < a)**, etc.

**Approximation** of **binomial** when **np** and **n(1−p)** large enough (**continuity correction** if **discrete** → **normal** — as **taught**).

---

## 6. Sampling

**Simple random**, **systematic**, **stratified** — **bias** reduction.  
**Central Limit** idea (awareness): **means** of **large** samples **approximately normal**.

---

## 7. Hypothesis tests (where examined)

**Null H₀** vs **alternative H₁**; **p-value** or **critical region**; **significance level** **α**.

**Type I** (reject true H₀) vs **Type II** (fail to reject false H₀).

---

## Common mistakes

- **Histogram** drawn with **wrong** **vertical** scale (**frequency** vs **density**).  
- **Confusing** **P(X > a)** with **P(X < a)** — **sketch** **curve**.  
- **Correlation** **≠** **causation**.
