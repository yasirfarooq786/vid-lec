# Complete Lecture: Vectors and Transformations (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**Vectors** describe **displacement**, **velocity**, **forces** (Mechanics), and **3D geometry** (**Pure**). **Linear transformations** include **matrices** (where examined). Programme: [Cambridge 9709](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-mathematics-9709/).

## 1. Vector representation

**Column vector** **(x, y)** or **(x, y, z)**; **position vector** **OP** = **r**.

**Magnitude |a| = √(x² + y²)** or **√(x² + y² + z²)**

**Unit vector** **â = a / |a|**

---

## 2. Operations

**a + b** (parallelogram / triangle rule)  
**λa** — **scalar multiple** (**parallel** if **b = λa**, λ ≠ 0)

**Components add:** **(x₁, y₁) + (x₂, y₂) = (x₁+x₂, y₁+y₂)**

---

## 3. Scalar (dot) product

**a · b = |a||b| cos θ** (θ angle between directions)

**Component form (2D/3D):** **a · b = a₁b₁ + a₂b₂ (+ a₃b₃)**

**Properties:** **a · a = |a|²**; **a · b = 0** ⟺ **perpendicular** (non-zero vectors)

**Angle:** **cos θ = (a · b)/(|a||b|)**

---

## 4. Lines in 2D/3D

**Vector equation:** **r = a + λb** (**a** point on line, **b** direction)

**Cartesian** from **parametric** by **eliminating** λ.

**Parallel lines:** **direction** vectors **scalar multiples**.  
**Skew lines** (3D): **not parallel** and **do not intersect** — test with **simultaneous** equations.

---

## 5. Shortest distance (awareness)

**Point to line** using **perpendicular** **vector** projection; **common perpendicular** of **two skew lines** (P3 style problems).

---

## 6. Transformations and matrices (if in your route)

**2×2** matrix **M** maps **(x, y)** → **M(x, y)**.

**Reflection**, **rotation**, **enlargement**, **shear** — **determinant** gives **area scale factor** (sign = **orientation**).

**Composition** = **matrix multiplication** (**right** matrix **first** applied? **Careful**: **BA** means **apply A then B** on **column** vectors **if** **v' = BAv** convention — **always** match **textbook**).

---

## Common mistakes

- **Confusing** **position** vector with **displacement**.  
- **Dot** vs **cross** product (**9709** **dot** emphasis unless **Further**).  
- **Direction** vector **zero** — **invalid**.
