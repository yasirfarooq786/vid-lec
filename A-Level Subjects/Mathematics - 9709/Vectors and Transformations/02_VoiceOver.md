# Voice-Over Script: Vectors and Transformations (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Arrow from O to P — position vector r]**

**Narrator:**  
A **vector** has **magnitude** and **direction**. **Position vector** **OP** fixes a **point** from the **origin**. **Adding** vectors is **walking** the **displacements** **head to tail**.

---

**[Visual: Dot product — projection of a onto b]**

**Narrator:**  
The **scalar product** **a dot b** equals **mod a mod b cosine theta**. In **components**, **multiply matching** **components** and **sum**. If the **dot product** is **zero**, vectors are **perpendicular**—unless one is **zero**.

---

**[Visual: Line r = a + λb — point slides along direction]**

**Narrator:**  
A **straight line** in **vector** form is **fixed point a** plus **lambda** times **direction b**. **Parallel** lines have **parallel** **direction** vectors. In **three D**, lines can **miss** each other entirely—**skew**.

---

**[Visual: 2×2 matrix acting on square grid]**

**Narrator:**  
**Matrices** can **encode** **transformations**: **rotations**, **reflections**, **stretches**. **Multiply** matrices to **compose** **maps**—watch **order** carefully against your **course** convention.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — magnitude, dot product, line equation, matrices]**

**Narrator:**  
Vectors unify **geometry** and **mechanics**. Practise **3D** **lines** until **algebra** feels as **comfortable** as **2D**. Next: **probability**.
