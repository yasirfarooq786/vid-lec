# Voice-Over Script: Coordinate Geometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Two points — distance formula Pythagoras]**

**Narrator:**  
**Coordinate geometry** turns **pictures** into **algebra**. **Distance** is **Pythagoras** on the **difference** in **x** and **y**. The **midpoint** **averages** the **coordinates**.

---

**[Visual: Parallel lines same slope; perpendicular L-shape]**

**Narrator:**  
**Parallel** lines share **gradient**. **Perpendicular** gradients **multiply** to **negative one**—unless one line is **vertical**, then think **special cases**.

---

**[Visual: Circle — centre dot, radius arrow, equation (x−a)²+(y−b)²=r²]**

**Narrator:**  
A **circle** is **all points** **fixed distance** from the **centre**. **Expand** carefully if you start from **general** form—**complete the square** to read off **centre** and **radius**.

---

**[Visual: Line cutting circle — discriminant popup]**

**Narrator:**  
Substitute the **line** into the **circle**. A **quadratic** emerges: **two** roots mean **two** **intersections**, **one** root means **tangency**, **none** means **miss**.

---

**[Visual: Tangent touching — radius perpendicular to tangent]**

**Narrator:**  
**Radius** to a **point of contact** is **perpendicular** to the **tangent**. That **right angle** shortcut often **beats** heavy **algebra**.

---

**Narrator:**  
**Sketch** every **geometry** question. **Label** **known** **points** and **read** **symmetry**. Next: **plane** **geometry** facts and **mensuration**.
