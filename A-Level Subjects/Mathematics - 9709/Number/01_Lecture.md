# Complete Lecture: Number (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

This module covers **number skills** that underpin **Pure Mathematics** and **Probability & Statistics**: **indices**, **surds**, **standard form**, **rounding**, and **sequences** foundations. Official programme: [Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics (9709)](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-mathematics-9709/).

---

## 1. Indices (exponent laws)

For **a > 0** and **m, n ∈ ℝ** (where defined):

- **aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ**  
- **aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ**  
- **(aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ**  
- **a⁰ = 1** (a ≠ 0)  
- **a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ**  
- **a^(1/q)** = **q**th root of **a**; **a^(p/q) = (q√a)ᵖ**

**Fractional bases and negatives:** work **step by step**; **even** roots need **non-negative** radicand in **ℝ**.

**Exam tip:** Simplify **entire expression** before substituting numerics; **common denominator** for **fractional** exponents.

---

## 2. Surds (√ form, rationalising)

**Surd** = irrational root left in exact form (**√2**, **√3**, …).

**Rules:** **√(ab) = √a × √b** (a, b ≥ 0); **√(a/b) = √a / √b**.

**Rationalise denominator:**

- **1/(a + √b)** → multiply **top and bottom** by **(a − √b)** (**conjugate**).  
- Denominator becomes **a² − b** (difference of squares).

**Simplify:** **√12 = 2√3**; **√(50) = 5√2**.

---

## 3. Standard form (scientific notation)

**a × 10ᵏ** with **1 ≤ |a| < 10**, **k ∈ ℤ**.

Used for **very large/small** quantities; **calculator** **ENG** key helps; **watch** **significant figures** in **applied** questions.

---

## 4. Accuracy and estimation

- **Decimal places (d.p.)** vs **significant figures (s.f.)** — follow **question** wording.  
- **Upper/lower bounds** from **rounded** data: e.g. value **to nearest cm** → **±0.5 cm** on **continuous** interpretation (exam may define **bounds** precisely).

---

## 5. Sequences — arithmetic preview

**nth term:** **uₙ = a + (n − 1)d**  
**Sum of n terms:** **Sₙ = n/2 (2a + (n−1)d)** or **n/2 (a + l)**.

*(Full **AP/GP** and **convergence** are developed in **Algebra** / **series** papers; this is the **computational** core.)*

---

## 6. Binomial coefficients (integer n, positive)

**(a + b)ⁿ** expansion uses **ⁿCᵣ** (also written **(n r)** or **n choose r**).

**ⁿCᵣ = n! / (r!(n−r)!)** — symmetry **ⁿCᵣ = ⁿCₙ₋ᵣ**.

Pascal’s triangle for **small n**; **general term** **ⁿCᵣ aⁿ⁻ʳ bʳ**.

---

## 7. Problem-solving habits

1. **Identify** **structure** (power, root, product).  
2. **Apply** **one law** at a time; **check** **domain** (e.g. **log** later requires **positive** arguments).  
3. **Final answer** in **simplest surd** / **index** form unless **decimal** requested.

---

## Common mistakes

- **(a + b)² ≠ a² + b²** — expand properly.  
- **√(x²) = |x|** in general on **ℝ** (sign matters in **equations**).  
- **Wrong rationalising** — multiply by **conjugate**, **not** random expression.
