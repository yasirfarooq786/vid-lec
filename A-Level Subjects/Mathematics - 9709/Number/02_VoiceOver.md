# Voice-Over Script: Number (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Title — “9709: Number — indices, surds, precision”]**

**Narrator:**  
Number work in **9709** is about **exact** and **efficient** manipulation. **Indices** let you **compress** repeated multiplication; the **laws** let you **simplify** before you touch a calculator.

---

**[Visual: Laws of indices animated — same base add exponents]**

**Narrator:**  
When you **multiply** powers of the same base, **add** exponents. **Divide**, **subtract**. **Power of a power**, **multiply** exponents. **Negative** exponent means **reciprocal**; **fractional** exponent means **root** then **power**, or **power** then **root**—be consistent and **check** **positivity** when you need **real** roots.

---

**[Visual: √12 splitting to 2√3; rationalising 1/(2+√3)]**

**Narrator:**  
**Surds** stay **exact**. **Factor** the radicand to **pull out** **square** factors. To **rationalise** a denominator like **two plus root three**, multiply **top and bottom** by the **conjugate** **two minus root three**. The denominator becomes a **rational** integer.

---

**[Visual: 3.42 × 10⁴ — standard form]**

**Narrator:**  
**Standard form** is **a times ten to the k** with **a** between **one** and **ten**. Use it for **scale** and for **checking** **order of magnitude** when a problem looks **messy**.

---

**[Visual: AP staircase — a, a+d, a+2d…]**

**Narrator:**  
**Arithmetic progressions** appear everywhere in **series** questions. **Nth term** is **a plus (n minus one)d**; **sum** uses **first plus last**, times **n over two**. Memorise **both** forms.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — indices, surds, binomial coefficient]**

**Narrator:**  
Speed comes from **pattern recognition**. Drill **mixed** **index** and **surd** questions until **algebra** feels **lighter**. Next: **Algebra and Graphs**.
