# Voice-Over Script: Algebra and Graphs (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Parabola — discriminant colour zones]**

**Narrator:**  
**Quadratics** are the **backbone** of **9709**. The **discriminant** tells you **how many** **real** **roots** you get. **Completing the square** gives the **turning point** instantly—essential for **sketches** and **optimisation**.

---

**[Visual: Number line — modulus |x−3| < 2 as interval]**

**Narrator:**  
Treat **inequalities** like **equations** but **flip** the sign when you **multiply** or **divide** by a **negative**. **Modulus** is **distance**: **mod x minus a less than b** becomes **x** trapped **between** **a minus b** and **a plus b**.

---

**[Visual: Polynomial long division — remainder box]**

**Narrator:**  
The **remainder theorem** says dividing by **x minus a** leaves remainder **P of a**. If **P of a** is **zero**, **x minus a** is a **factor**. Use that to **solve** **cubics** and to **strip** factors before **partial fractions**.

---

**[Visual: Transformation animation — y = f(x) vs y = 2f(x) vs y = f(2x)]**

**Narrator:**  
**Stretch** **vertically** by **k** multiplies **outputs** by **k**. **Stretch horizontally** by **factor one over k** replaces **x** with **k x**. **Plus inside** the bracket shifts **opposite** to what you **see** on the axis—**f of x plus two** moves **left** **two**.

---

**[Visual: y = e^x and y = ln x reflected in y = x]**

**Narrator:**  
**Exponentials** and **logarithms** are **inverses**. Their **graphs** mirror in **y equals x**. **Domain** of **ln** is **strictly positive**—always **check** **arguments** when you **solve** **log** equations.

---

**[Visual: Tangent touching curve — derivative as gradient]**

**Narrator:**  
**Differentiation** gives **gradient** of a **curve**. **Chain rule** peels **layers** like **sine of three x**. **Product** and **quotient** rules handle **products** and **ratios**. Set **derivative** **zero** for **stationary** points; use **second derivative** or **sign table** to **classify** **max** or **min**.

---

**[Visual: Shaded area under curve between a and b]**

**Narrator:**  
**Integration** **reverses** differentiation up to **plus c**. A **definite integral** from **a** to **b** is the **limit** of **sums**—for **area**, watch **signs** if the **curve** crosses the **axis**.

---

**Narrator:**  
Every **pure** paper **touches** this topic. **Sketch** often, **label** **asymptotes**, and **state** **domains** explicitly. Next: **coordinate geometry**.
