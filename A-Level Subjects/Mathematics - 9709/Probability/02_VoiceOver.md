# Voice-Over Script: Probability (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Venn diagram — A union B shaded]**

**Narrator:**  
**Probability** measures **likelihood** on a **zero to one** scale. For **either A or B**, add **individual** probabilities then **subtract** the **overlap** so you **don’t double count**.

---

**[Visual: Tree diagram — branches with p and 1−p]**

**Narrator:**  
**Conditional probability** answers **given that B happened, how likely is A**. On a **tree**, **multiply** along a **path** for **joint** events. **Independent** events **multiply** **without** **adjusting** **conditions**.

---

**[Visual: nCr formula with calculator button]**

**Narrator:**  
When outcomes are **equally likely**, **probability** is **favourable** over **total**. **Combinations** count **selections** where **order** **doesn’t** matter—**n choose r**. **Permutations** care about **order**.

---

**[Visual: Binomial histogram — n=10, p=0.3]**

**Narrator:**  
The **binomial** model needs **fixed n**, **constant p**, **independent** **trials**, and **two** **outcomes**. **Expectation** is **n p**; **variance** is **n p one minus p**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — conditional, independence, counting, binomial]**

**Narrator:**  
Read **with** or **without** **replacement** **every** time. **Bayes**-style questions want **clear** **fraction** **arithmetic**—show **numerator** and **denominator**. Next: **statistics**.
