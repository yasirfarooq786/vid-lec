# Complete Lecture: Geometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**Synthetic geometry** (angles, parallel lines, polygons, similarity) supports **reasoning** in **pure** and **mechanics** contexts and **diagram-based** problems. ## 1. Angle facts

- **Angles on a straight line** sum to **180°**.  
- **Angles at a point** sum to **360°**.  
- **Vertically opposite** angles are **equal**.  
- **Parallel lines** with a **transversal:** **corresponding** angles **equal**; **alternate** angles **equal**; **interior** angles **supplementary** (**sum 180°**).

---

## 2. Triangle properties

- **Angle sum** **180°**.  
- **Exterior angle** = **sum** of **two** **interior** **opposite** angles.  
- **Isosceles:** **two** **equal** sides → **two** **equal** **base** angles.

**Congruence:** **SSS**, **SAS**, **ASA**, **RHS** (right angle, hypotenuse, side).

**Similarity:** **equal** **angles** (AA) → **corresponding** sides **in proportion**.

---

## 3. Pythagoras’ theorem

**Right-angled triangle:** **a² + b² = c²** (**c** **hypotenuse**).

**Converse:** if **a² + b² = c²**, angle opposite **c** is **90°**.

---

## 4. Circle theorems (selection — verify with your syllabus depth)

- **Angle at centre** = **2 ×** **angle at circumference** (same **arc**).  
- **Angles in same segment** are **equal**.  
- **Angle in semicircle** = **90°**.  
- **Cyclic quadrilateral:** **opposite** angles **sum** to **180°**.  
- **Tangent ⟂ radius** at **point of contact**.  
- **Alternate segment** theorem (tangent–chord angle).

---

## 5. Polygons

**Sum of interior angles** of **n-gon:** **(n − 2) × 180°**  
**Regular n-gon** each **interior angle:** **(n − 2) × 180° / n**

---

## 6. Area and length links

**Triangle area:** **½ × base × height**; also **½ ab sin C** (**trigonometry** link).

**Similar figures:** **length ratio k** → **area ratio k²** → **volume ratio k³**.

---

## 7. Coordinate connection

Many “**geometry**” questions are **solved faster** with **coordinates** or **vectors**—choose the **tool** the **question** suggests.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming **diagram** is **drawn to scale** unless **stated**.  
- **Wrong** **corresponding** sides in **similar** triangles.
