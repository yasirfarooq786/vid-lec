# Voice-Over Script: Mensuration (Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709)

**[Visual: Protractor split — 180° = π rad]**

**Narrator:**  
**Radians** measure **angles** by **arc length per radius**. **Half a turn** is **pi** radians, a **full turn** **two pi**. In **calculus**, **trig** derivatives are **clean** **only** in **radians**—examiners assume that unless they say otherwise.

---

**[Visual: Sector pizza slice — s = rθ, A = ½r²θ]**

**Narrator:**  
**Arc length** is **radius times angle** when **angle** is in **radians**. **Sector area** is **half r squared theta**—same **theta** rule. If the question gives **degrees**, **convert** **first**.

---

**[Visual: Cone and sphere formulas]**

**Narrator:**  
**Cylinder** volume is **pi r squared h**. **Cone** is **one third** of that. **Sphere** is **four thirds pi r cubed**. **Surface area** of a **sphere** is **four pi r squared**—memorise or **derive** from **question** data.

---

**[Visual: Curve spun around x-axis — disk method hint]**

**Narrator:**  
When a **graph** spins around an **axis**, **integration** stacks **thin discs**. **Volume** is **pi** times the **integral** of **y squared** **dx** for **rotation** about the **x-axis**—you’ll use this **heavily** in **pure**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — radians, arc/sector, solids, revolution]**

**Narrator:**  
Always **write** the **unit**—**radians** or **degrees**—in **final** answers when **angles** matter. Next: **trigonometry**.
