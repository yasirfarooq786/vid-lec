# Voice-Over Script: Chemical Reactions (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Reaction types montage — precipitate, gas, colour change]**

**Narrator:**  
Chemical reactions **rearrange** atoms. Learn to **classify** them: **acid–base**, **precipitation**, **redox**, and more. **Redox** is everywhere in **halogens**, **metals**, and **electrochemistry**.

---

**[Visual: Oxidation numbers on a molecule — before/after]**

**Narrator:**  
**Oxidation number** tracks **electron** **control**. **Increase** means **oxidation**; **decrease** means **reduction**. The **species oxidised** is the **reducing agent** because it **donates** electrons to something else.

---

**[Visual: Two half-equations — electrons equalised and added]**

**Narrator:**  
Balance tough **redox** by **half-equations**: balance **atoms**, then **charges** with **electrons** and **H+** or **OH-** as your **medium** requires, then match **electron** counts and **add**.

---

**[Visual: Reversible arrow — closed flask]**

**Narrator:**  
Many reactions are **reversible**. At **equilibrium**, **forward** and **backward** rates **match**—concentrations stay **constant** but **not** necessarily **equal**. You’ll quantify that with **Kc** and **Kp** later.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — redox identification, half-equations, agents]**

**Narrator:**  
Drill **oxidation numbers** on **every** **exam** **equation**. Next: **practical** **techniques**.
