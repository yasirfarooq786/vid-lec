## Types of chemical change

- **Synthesis / addition:** A + B → AB  
- **Decomposition:** AB → A + B  
- **Displacement:** A + BC → AC + B  
- **Precipitation:** ions combine to form **insoluble** solid  
- **Acid–base / neutralisation**  
- **Redox:** **electron transfer** (includes many **combustion** and **displacement** reactions)

---

## Oxidation numbers (oxidation states)

Rules (summary):

- **Element** in standard state: **0**  
- **Monatomic ion:** equals **charge**  
- **Group 1** in compounds: **+1**; **Group 2:** **+2**  
- **F** in compounds: **−1**; **O** usually **−2** (except peroxide, superoxide, OF₂)  
- **H** usually **+1** (except **hydrides**)  
- **Sum** in a **neutral** compound = **0**; in a **polyatomic ion** = **ion charge**

**Oxidation** = **increase** in oxidation number (**loss** of electrons).  
**Reduction** = **decrease** (**gain** of electrons).

**Reducing agent** is **oxidised**; **oxidising agent** is **reduced**.

---

## Balancing redox (acidic / alkaline media)

Half-equation method:

1. Assign **oxidation numbers**; identify **oxidised** and **reduced** species.  
2. Write **half-equations** with **electrons**; balance **atoms** (add **H₂O**, **H⁺** in **acid**; **OH⁻** / **H₂O** in **alkali** as taught).  
3. Equalise **electrons**; **add** half-equations.

## Energetics link (preview)

**Exothermic:** products **lower** enthalpy than reactants (**ΔH < 0**).  
**Endothermic:** **ΔH > 0**.

**Activation energy** determines **rate**, not only **ΔH** (see **kinetics**).

## Reversible reactions

**⇌** symbol; **dynamic equilibrium** when **forward** and **reverse** rates **equal** (Topic **equilibria**).

## Common misconceptions

- **Oxidation** does **not** require **oxygen**—any **increase** in **oxidation number**.  
- **Catalyst** changes **rate** but **not** **ΔH** or **equilibrium position** (equilibria topic).
