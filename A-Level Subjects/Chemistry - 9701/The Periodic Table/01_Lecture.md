## Structure of the table

- **Periods** 1–7: **principal quantum number** of **outer shell** increases.  
- **Groups** 1–18 (or **1–8** traditional labelling): **valence** **electron** count **patterns**.  
- **s-, p-, d-** blocks from **orbital** filling.

---

## Trends across Period 3 (typical AS data)

**Left → right:**

| Property | Trend | Reason (brief) |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| **Atomic radius** | **Decreases** | **Same** shell; **greater** **nuclear charge** pulls **electrons** **in** |
| **Ionisation energy** | **Generally increases** | **Harder** to remove **electron** from **smaller**, **more attracted** orbital |
| **Electronegativity** | **Increases** | **Greater** **pull** on **bonding** electrons |
| **Melting point** (metals → giant structures) | **Si** maximum (giant **covalent**); then **lower** for **simple molecular** **P₄, S₈, Cl₂, Ar** | **Bonding** **type** and **structure** change |

---

## Group 2 — alkaline earth metals

- **Electronic configuration:** **ns²**  
- **Reactivity** **increases** **down** group (**lower** **IE**; **easier** **M → M²⁺**).  
- Reactions with **water** / **steam**; **oxides** **basic**; **hydroxides** **sparingly** soluble **down** group (**some exceptions** in **solubility** detail).

---

## Group 17 — halogens

- **ns² np⁵**; **diatomic** **X₂**.  
- **Reactivity** **decreases** **down** group (**weaker** **oxidising** agents).  
- **Displacement:** more **reactive** halogen **oxidises** **halide** ions below it.

---

## Transition metals (overview)

- **Variable oxidation states** (e.g. **Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺**, **Cu⁺/Cu²⁺**).  
- **Coloured** **complex ions**; **catalyst** properties (**V₂O₅**, **Fe** in **Haber**).  
- **Denser**, **higher** **melting points** than **s-block** (generalisation).

---

## Period 3 chlorides and oxides (structures)

- **Ionic** **giant** structures **left** (NaCl, MgO) → **high** **mp**;  
- **Al₂Cl₆** **covalent** **dimer** **vapour**; **SiO₂** **giant covalent**;  
- **P, S** **molecular** oxides/chlorides; **simple molecular** **low** **mp**.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Third IE** of **Na** is **huge** (removing **inner** shell **2p**)—**not** same trend as **first IE** across period.  
- **Lower** **atomic radius** does **not** always mean **higher** **mp**—**structure** **dominates**.
