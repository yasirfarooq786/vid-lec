# Voice-Over Script: The Periodic Table (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Period 3 row — atomic radius arrows shrinking]**

**Narrator:**  
Across **period three**, **atomic radius** **shrinks** because **protons** are added while **new** electrons enter the **same** **shell**—**shielding** barely changes but **nuclear charge** climbs. **Ionisation energy** and **electronegativity** **generally rise**.

---

**[Visual: Melting point graph — peak at silicon]**

**Narrator:**  
**Melting points** track **structure**. **Metallic** **sodium** and **magnesium** are **giant**. **Aluminium** is **metallic** too. **Silicon** is **giant covalent**—very **high** melting. **Phosphorus**, **sulfur**, **chlorine**, and **argon** are **simple molecular** or **atomic**—**much lower** melting.

---

**[Visual: Group 2 metals reacting — trend down group]**

**Narrator:**  
**Group two** gets **more reactive** **down** the group—**outer** **s** electrons are **further** from the **nucleus** and **easier** to **lose**. **Hydroxide solubility** trends matter for **precipitation** questions.

---

**[Visual: Halogen displacement in test tubes]**

**Narrator:**  
**Halogens** become **less oxidising** **down** the group. **Chlorine** **displaces** **bromide** and **iodide**; **bromine** **displaces** **iodide** but **not** **chloride**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — trends, Group 2, Group 17, Period 3 structures]**

**Narrator:**  
Learn to **justify** every **trend** with **nuclear charge**, **distance**, and **shielding**. Next: **metals** in **industry** and **reactivity**.
