## Group 2 carbonates

**General formula MCO₃** (M = Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba).

**With acid:** **CO₂** **effervescence** + **salt** + **water**  
**MCO₃ + 2H⁺ → M²⁺ + CO₂ + H₂O**

---

## Thermal stability trend

**Down** the group, **carbonates** become **more thermally stable** (**higher** **temperature** needed to decompose).

**Explanation:** **smaller** **M²⁺** has **higher charge density** and **polarises** the **CO₃²⁻** ion **more**, **distorting** the anion and **weakening** **C–O** bonds → **MgCO₃** decomposes at a **lower** temperature than **BaCO₃**.

*(Cambridge teaching: **carbonate thermal stability increases down Group 2**; **cation polarising power decreases** as **ionic radius** increases.)*

**Decomposition:** **MCO₃ → MO + CO₂** (high T)

---

## Group 2 nitrates (related)

**Nitrates** also decompose on heating: **nitrogen dioxide**, **oxygen**, **metal oxide** (patterns examined with **carbonates**).

**Thermal stability of nitrates increases down the group** (less **readily** decomposed **at bottom**—**confirm** **wording** in **your** notes).

---

## Limestone cycle (synoptic)

1. **CaCO₃** **heated** in **kiln** → **CaO** (**lime**) + **CO₂**  
2. **CaO** + **H₂O** → **Ca(OH)₂** (**slaked lime**)  
3. **Ca(OH)₂** + **CO₂** → **CaCO₃** + **H₂O** (or with **aqueous** **CO₂**/carbonic acid)

Used in **construction**, **agriculture** (**pH**), **flue gas** **desulfurisation**.

---

## Practical identification

**Limewater** (**Ca(OH)₂** **aq**) **turns milky** with **CO₂** (**CaCO₃** **precipitate**).

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Group 1** **carbonates** (except **Li**) are **more stable** than **Group 2** analogues—**do not** **assume** **same** **trend** **between** **groups**.  
- **Thermal decomposition** is **not** the same as **dissolving** in **acid**.
