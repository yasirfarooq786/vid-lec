# Voice-Over Script: Carbonates (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Group 2 carbonates — fizz with acid]**

**Narrator:**  
**Group two carbonates** react with **acids** to release **carbon dioxide**, **water**, and a **salt**. It is a classic **test** for **carbonate** ions.

---

**[Visual: Bunsen heating MgCO3 vs BaCO3 — temperature labels]**

**Narrator:**  
When **heated**, **carbonates** break down to the **metal oxide** and **carbon dioxide**. **Magnesium carbonate** needs a **lower** temperature than **barium carbonate**. **Thermal stability increases down the group** because **larger** cations **polarise** the carbonate ion **less**.

---

**[Visual: Lime kiln — CaCO3 to CaO + CO2]**

**Narrator:**  
The **limestone cycle** rotates **calcium carbonate** through **lime** and **slaked lime**, absorbing and releasing **carbon dioxide**. It matters for **industry** and **acid gas** **scrubbing**.

---

**[Visual: Limewater + CO2 — milky]**

**Narrator:**  
**Carbon dioxide** turns **limewater** **cloudy**—**calcium carbonate** **precipitates**. Use that **test** in **qualitative** work.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — acid reaction, thermal trend, limestone cycle, CO2 test]**

**Narrator:**  
Pair **trends** with **ionic size** and **polarisation**. Next: **sulfur** chemistry.
