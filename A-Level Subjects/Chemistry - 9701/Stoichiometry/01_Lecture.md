# Complete Lecture: Stoichiometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**Stoichiometry** links **balanced equations** to **masses**, **gas volumes**, and **solution concentrations** via the **mole**. Programme: [Cambridge 9701](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-chemistry-9701/).

---

## The mole

**One mole** contains **Avogadro constant** **L** ≈ **6.02 × 10²³** entities (atoms, molecules, ions, formula units).

**n = mass / Mr** (or **Ar** for atoms); **Mr** is **dimensionless** on the **relative** scale; **molar mass** **M** in **g mol⁻¹** numerically equals **Mr**.

---

## Balanced equations

Coefficients give **mole ratios** of **reactants** and **products**.

**Example:** **2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O** → **2 mol** H₂ reacts with **1 mol** O₂ to give **2 mol** H₂O.

**Ionic equations** (full and **net**) omit **spectator ions** where appropriate.

---

## Mass–mass calculations

1. Write **balanced** equation.  
2. Convert **given mass** → **moles** (**÷ Mr**).  
3. Use **mole ratio** to find **moles** of **target** substance.  
4. Convert **moles** → **mass** (**× Mr**).

**Limiting reagent:** the reactant **fully consumed** first **limits** product **mole** amount.

---

## Gas stoichiometry

Use **pV = nRT** or **molar volume** under **stated** conditions.

**n = V / Vm** when **Vm** given (e.g. **24 dm³ mol⁻¹** at RTP in many questions).

---

## Solution stoichiometry

**Molar concentration:** **c = n / V** (mol dm⁻³).

**Titrations:** **c₁V₁ / c₂V₂ = n₁/n₂** for **acid–base** when **balanced** **1:1** etc.; use **mole ratio** from **equation** in general.

**n = c × V** (V in **dm³**).

---

## Percentage composition and formulae

- **Empirical formula** from **composition** or **combustion** data: divide **masses** by **Ar** → **simplest** **whole-number** ratio.
- **Molecular formula** = **(empirical)ₙ** where **n = Mr molecular / Mr empirical**.

---

## Percentage yield and purity

**% yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100%**

**% purity** from **titration** or **mass** of **active** component.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Mr** is **not** “grams”—it’s **relative**; **molar mass** carries **units** **g mol⁻¹**.
- **Volume** in **c = n/V** must be **dm³** for **mol dm⁻³** (divide **cm³** by **1000**).
