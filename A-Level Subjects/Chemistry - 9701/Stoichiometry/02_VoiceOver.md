# Voice-Over Script: Stoichiometry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Mole as counting unit — Avogadro number]**

**Narrator:**  
The **mole** links the **microscopic** world to the **lab**. One mole is **six point zero two times ten to the twenty-three** particles. **Mass** divided by **molar mass** gives **amount** in **moles**.

---

**[Visual: Balanced equation — mole ratio arrows]**

**Narrator:**  
**Coefficients** in a **balanced** equation are **mole ratios**. Convert **what you know** to **moles**, **scale** by the **ratio**, then convert back to **grams** or **volume** as asked.

---

**[Visual: Titration — c1V1 with mole ratio box]**

**Narrator:**  
In **titrations**, **moles** of **acid** and **base** match the **stoichiometry** of the **equation**. **Concentration** times **volume** in **decimetres cubed** gives **moles**. Watch for **diprotic** acids and **2:1** ratios.

---

**[Visual: Limiting reagent — one flask empty first]**

**Narrator:**  
If two **reactants** are mixed, the one that **runs out** first is **limiting**—it **caps** the **product** you can make. The other is **in excess**.

---
