## Enthalpy (H)

**Heat energy** at **constant pressure**; **ΔH** = **H(products) − H(reactants)**.

- **Exothermic:** **ΔH < 0** (releases heat).  
- **Endothermic:** **ΔH > 0** (absorbs heat).

**Standard conditions:** **298 K**, **100 kPa** (or **1 bar**—use **data booklet**), **substances** in **standard states**.

---

## Key definitions

- **ΔHc°:** **enthalpy of combustion** (per **mole** substance **burned** **completely** in **O₂**).  
- **ΔHf°:** **enthalpy of formation** (1 **mol** compound from **elements** in **standard states**).  
- **ΔHneut°:** **acid–base** neutralisation.  
- **ΔHsol°:** **solution** (lattice + hydration components in **analysis**).

---

## Hess’s law

**ΔH** for a reaction is **independent** of **pathway**—same as **conservation of energy** in **chemical** context.

**Cycle method:** construct **triangle** or **polygon** of **known** **ΔH** values; **unknown** = **sum** of **steps** **with signs**.

**Formation cycle:** **ΔH reaction = Σ ΔHf°(products) − Σ ΔHf°(reactants)**.

---

## Bond energies (mean bond enthalpies)

**ΔH ≈ Σ(bonds broken) − Σ(bonds formed)** (gas phase, **average** bond enthalpies—**approximate**).

**Limitation:** **mean** values differ from **exact** **bond** in a **specific** molecule.

---

## Born–Haber cycle (ionic solids)

Relates **lattice formation enthalpy**, **ionisation energies**, **electron affinity**, **atomisation**, **bond** **dissociation** for **non-metals**, **formation** **enthalpy**.

**Lattice enthalpy** **exothermic** (definition: **gaseous ions → solid**—**sign** **convention** **learn** from **booklet**).

---

## Calorimetry link

**q = mcΔT**; scale to **per mole**; **systematic** **heat loss**.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Bond breaking** is **endothermic**; **bond making** is **exothermic**.  
- **ΔHf°** for **elements** in **standard states** = **0** **by definition**.
