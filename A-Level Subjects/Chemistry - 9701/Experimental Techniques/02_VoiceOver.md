# Voice-Over Script: Experimental Techniques (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Burette and conical flask — meniscus close-up]**

**Narrator:**  
**Titrations** need a **clean** **burette**, a **volumetric pipette** for the **analyte**, and **consistent** **endpoint** detection. Read the **meniscus** at **eye level** and **repeat** until **concordant** titres agree within about **point one cubic centimetre**.

---

**[Visual: Chromatogram — Rf as ratio]**

**Narrator:**  
**Chromatography** separates by **partition** between **moving** and **fixed** phases. **Rf** compares how far a **spot** travels versus the **solvent front**—use it to **match** unknowns to **standards**.

---

**[Visual: Polystyrene cup calorimeter — thermometer]**

**Narrator:**  
**Calorimetry** estimates **heat** with **q equals m c delta T**. Real setups **lose** heat to the room, so **magnitude** of **enthalpy change** is often **underestimated** for **exothermic** work unless **insulation** improves.

---

**[Visual: Safety symbols wall]**

**Narrator:**  
Always tie **observations** to **risk**: **gloves** and **goggles** for **corrosives**, **ventilation** for **toxic** **vapours**. **Record** **raw** data **first**, then **process**.
