# Voice-Over Script: Sulfur (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: S8 crown structure]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sulfur** commonly exists as **S eight** rings. It forms **dioxide** and **trioxide**, key **industrial** **intermediates**.

---

**[Visual: SO2 as bent molecule — lone pair on sulfur]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sulfur dioxide** dissolves to give **acidic** solutions. It is a **reducing agent** in many **bleaching** contexts and a **major** **air pollutant** when **burned** from **fossil** fuels or **ore** processing.

---

**[Visual: Contact process flow — catalyst bed, absorption tower]**

**Narrator:**  
The **Contact process** **oxidises** **SO two** to **SO three** over a **vanadium five oxide** **catalyst**. **SO three** is **absorbed** in **concentrated sulfuric acid** to make **oleum**, then **diluted**—**not** by **dumping** into **water** directly.

---

**[Visual: Fertiliser bags — sulfuric acid as precursor]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sulfuric acid** feeds **phosphate** **fertilisers**, **ammonium sulfate**, and many **chemical** **chains**. **Economies** track **acid** production as an **industrial** **indicator**.
