# Voice-Over Script: Organic Chemistry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Homologous series — methane to butane]**

**Narrator:**  
**Organic chemistry** is **carbon** **framework** plus **functional groups**. Learn **IUPAC** names: **chain**, **suffix** for the **group**, **numbers** for **positions**.

---

**[Visual: Cis vs trans alkene; mirror images for chiral centre]**

**Narrator:**  
**Structural isomers** share a **formula** but differ in **connectivity**. **Stereoisomers** differ in **space**: **cis and trans** around a **double bond**, or **optical** isomers when a **carbon** has **four different** **groups**.

---

**[Visual: Curly arrows — Br+ attacked by pi bond]**

**Narrator:**  
**Alkenes** react by **electrophilic addition**. **Curly arrows** flow from **electron-rich** to **electron-poor** regions. **Markovnikov** patterns come from **carbocation** **stability**.

---

**[Visual: SN2 backside attack — Walden inversion sketch]**

**Narrator:**  
**Halogenoalkanes** undergo **nucleophilic substitution** or **elimination** depending on **reagent** and **structure**. **Primary** substrates often favour **SN two**; **tertiary** often favour **SN one** in **polar** **protic** **solvents**.

---

**[Visual: Oxidation of primary alcohol — distillation vs reflux]**

**Narrator:**  
**Primary alcohols** become **aldehydes** then **acids** if you **oxidise** hard enough. **Distil off** the **aldehyde** to **stop** at the **first** stage. **Secondary** alcohols stop at **ketones**.

---

**[Visual: Benzene ring — electrophile NO2+ attacking]**

**Narrator:**  
**Benzene** resists **addition** but undergoes **electrophilic substitution** so the **ring** stays **aromatic**. **Phenol** is **more reactive** than **benzene** toward **electrophiles**.

---

**Narrator:**  
Use your **data booklet** for **spectra** and **reagents**. Practise **mechanism** **drawing** until every **arrow** has a **clear** **source** and **destination**. Next: **past paper** **strategy**.
