## Fundamental terms

- **Element:** atoms with the **same atomic number** **Z** (number of **protons**).
- **Compound:** **two or more elements** **chemically** combined in **fixed** proportion.
- **Mixture:** **physical** blend; composition **variable**.

---

## Subatomic particles

| Particle | Relative mass | Relative charge | Location |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Proton** | 1 | +1 | **Nucleus** |
| **Neutron** | 1 | 0 | **Nucleus** |
| **Electron** | ≈ 1/1836 | −1 | **Shells** / **orbitals** (outside nucleus) |

**Nucleon number (mass number) A** = protons + neutrons.

---

## Isotopes

Same **Z**, different **neutron count** → different **A**.

- **Chemical properties** nearly **identical** (same electron arrangement).
- **Physical properties** may differ slightly (mass, **bond vibration**).
- **Relative atomic mass** of an element is a **weighted mean** of isotope masses.

**Radioactive isotopes** used in **dating**, **tracers**, **medicine** (depth per syllabus).

---

## Electrons and quantisation

- **Energy levels** (shells) **n = 1, 2, 3 …**
- **Subshells:** **s, p, d, f** with **increasing** energy (order follows **Aufbau** diagram in data booklet).
- **Ground-state configuration** for **many** atoms: fill **lowest** energy **orbitals** first; **Hund’s rule** for **degenerate** orbitals; **Pauli**—max **two** electrons per orbital, **opposite spin**.

**Examples:**  
Na: **1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s¹**; Cl: **1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s² 3p⁵**.

**Ions:** **electrons** lost/gained to achieve **noble gas** configuration where **energetically** favourable (e.g. Na⁺, Cl⁻, Ca²⁺).

## Periodic table and classification

- **Period** = row (**increasing Z**); **group** = column (**similar** outer **valence** electrons).
- **Metals / non-metals / metalloids**—trends in **electronegativity**, **atomic radius**, **ionisation energy** developed in **The Periodic Table** topic.

## Chemical formulae

- **Molecular formula** (actual atom counts in a **molecule**).
- **Empirical formula** (simplest **ratio**).
- **Structural** / **displayed** formulae show **bonding** (organic topic).

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Isotope** difference is **neutrons**, **not** “more electrons.”
- **Relative atomic mass** is **not** the mass of **one** atom in **grams**—use **moles** for **macroscopic** mass.
