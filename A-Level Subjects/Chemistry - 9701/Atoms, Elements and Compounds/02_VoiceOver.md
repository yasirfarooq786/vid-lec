# Voice-Over Script: Atoms, Elements and Compounds (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Atom — nucleus with protons/neutrons, electron shells]**

**Narrator:**  
An **atom** has a **nucleus** of **protons** and **neutrons** and **electrons** outside. The **atomic number** counts **protons** and defines the **element**. The **mass number** counts **nucleons**.

---

**[Visual: Two isotopes of carbon — same Z, different A]**

**Narrator:**  
**Isotopes** share **chemistry** because they share **electron structure**, but differ in **mass**. **Relative atomic mass** on the periodic table is an **average** weighted by **natural abundance**.

---

**[Visual: Aufbau filling diagram — 1s, 2s, 2p…]**

**Narrator:**  
Electrons fill **subshells** by **increasing energy**. Use the **data booklet** order for **s, p, d**. **Hund’s rule** spreads electrons in **degenerate** **p** orbitals before **pairing**. **Ions** form when atoms **gain** or **lose** electrons to reach **stable** configurations.

---

**[Visual: Periodic table — period vs group highlighted]**

**Narrator:**  
**Groups** share **valence** **electron patterns**, which drives **similar reactions**. **Periods** show **shell** **building** from **left** to **right**.

---

Drill **electron configurations** until they are **automatic**. Next: **the mole** and **stoichiometry**.
