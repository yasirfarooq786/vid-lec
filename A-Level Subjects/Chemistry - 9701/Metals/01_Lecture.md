## Metallic bonding

**Lattice** of **positive ions** in a **sea of delocalised electrons**. Explains **electrical** and **thermal conductivity**, **malleability**, **ductility**, typically **high** **melting points** for **transition** metals.

---

## Reactivity series (reducing power)

**K Na Ca Mg Al (C) Zn Fe (H) Cu Ag Au** (typical order—learn **syllabus** version).

- Metals **above** **H** **displace** **H⁺** from **acid** (with **conditions**—**Al** **passivated** by **oxide** in **nitric** acid, etc.).  
- More **reactive** metal **displaces** less **reactive** from **aqueous** **ions** or **oxide**.

---

## Extraction

- **Electrolysis** for **very reactive** metals (**Na**, **Al** from **bauxite** via **cryolite** melt—**huge** **energy** cost).  
- **Carbon reduction** for **middle** reactivity (**Fe** in **blast furnace**: **C → CO** reduces **Fe₂O₃**; **limestone** removes **SiO₂** as **slag**).  
- **Less reactive** (**Cu**, **Ag**) may be **native** or **roasted** then **reduced** **easily**.

---

## Iron and steel

- **Blast furnace** products: **pig iron** (**high** **C**, **Si**, **P**, **S**).  
- **Basic oxygen** / **other** processes **oxidise** **impurities**; **alloys** (**stainless**—**Cr**, **Ni**) alter **properties**.

---

## Aluminium

- **Bauxite** → **purified** **Al₂O₃** → **Hall–Heroult** **electrolysis** in **molten** **cryolite** (**lowers** **mp**); **graphite** **anodes** **burn** to **CO₂** (awareness).

---

## Corrosion

- **Rust** (**hydrated** **iron** **oxides**) needs **O₂** + **H₂O**; **salt** **accelerates** (**electrochemical** **cells** on **surface**).  
- **Prevention:** **barrier** **coating**, **galvanising** (**Zn** **sacrificial**), **cathodic** **protection**.

---

## Transition metals (synoptic)

- **Catalysts** (**Fe** **Haber**, **Ni** **hydrogenation**).  
- **Variable** **oxidation** **states** and **coloured** **complexes**.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Carbon** is **not** a **metal** but appears in **reduction** **sequences**.  
- **Aluminium** looks **unreactive** because of **oxide** **layer**, not **low** **intrinsic** **reactivity**.
