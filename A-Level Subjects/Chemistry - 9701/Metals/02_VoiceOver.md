# Voice-Over Script: Metals (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Metallic lattice animation — ions in electron sea]**

**Narrator:**  
**Metallic bonding** is **positive ions** in a **sea of delocalised electrons**. That explains **conductivity** and **malleability**—layers can **slip** without **breaking** **attraction**.

---

**[Visual: Reactivity series ladder — displacement demo]**

**Narrator:**  
The **reactivity series** ranks **reducing power**. A **more reactive** metal **donates** electrons **more readily**, so it **displaces** **less reactive** metals from **solution** and **reacts** **more vigorously** with **acids**—where **oxide** films don’t block the surface.

---

**[Visual: Blast furnace diagram — iron ore, coke, limestone, slag]**

**Narrator:**  
**Iron** comes from **reduction** of **iron three oxide** in a **blast furnace** using **carbon monoxide** from **coke**. **Limestone** forms **slag** with **silicate** **impurities**. The product is **pig iron**—later **refined** to **steel**.

---

**[Visual: Electrolysis cell — molten aluminium]**

**Narrator:**  
**Aluminium** is **too reactive** for **carbon reduction** of its **oxide** at **economical** temperatures. **Electrolysis** of **alumina** dissolved in **molten cryolite** is the **industrial** route—**electricity** intensive.

---

**[Visual: Rusty nail vs galvanised]**

**Narrator:**  
**Rust** needs **oxygen** and **water**. **Zinc coating** **sacrifices** itself **preferentially**, **protecting** **steel**.

---

**Narrator:**  
Link **every** **metal** question to **electrons** and **redox**. Next: **electrolysis** and **cells**.
