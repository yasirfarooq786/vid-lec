# Voice-Over Script: Air and Water (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Pie chart — nitrogen, oxygen, argon]**

**Narrator:**  
**Dry air** is mostly **nitrogen** and **oxygen** with **argon** and **small** amounts of **carbon dioxide**. **Trace** gases and **water vapour** still matter for **weather** and **climate**.

---

**[Visual: Car exhaust → catalytic converter → cleaner gases]**

**Narrator:**  
**Engines** make **nitrogen oxides** and **carbon monoxide**. A **catalytic converter** uses **platinum**-group metals to **speed** **redox** steps that turn those pollutants into **nitrogen**, **carbon dioxide**, and **steam**.

---

**[Visual: Acid rain dissolving limestone statue]**

**Narrator:**  
**Sulfur** and **nitrogen** oxides form **strong acids** in **rain**, attacking **carbonate** **stone** and **acidifying** **lakes**. **Scrubbers** in **power plants** reduce **sulfur** emissions.

---

**[Visual: Kettle fur — temporary hardness]**

**Narrator:**  
**Hard water** contains **calcium** and **magnesium** ions. **Temporary hardness** is removed by **boiling** because **hydrogencarbonate** decomposes to **carbonate** **precipitate**. **Permanent hardness** needs **ion exchange** or other **methods**.

---

**[Visual: Water treatment plant flowchart]**

**Narrator:**  
**Drinking water** is **filtered** and **disinfected**, often with **chlorine**. **Sewage** treatment uses **physical**, **biological**, and **chemical** stages to **lower** **organic** load before **discharge**.

---
