# Voice-Over Script: Electricity and Chemistry (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Electrolysis vs cell — battery direction arrows]**

**Narrator:**  
In **electrolysis**, a **power supply** **forces** a reaction. In a **voltaic cell**, a **spontaneous** reaction **pushes** electrons through a **wire**. In **both**, **oxidation** is at the **anode** and **reduction** at the **cathode**—remember **AnOx** and **RedCat**.

---

**[Visual: Ions migrating — cations to cathode]**

**Narrator:**  
**Positive cations** move to the **negative cathode** and **gain electrons**. **Anions** travel to the **positive anode** and **lose electrons**. In **aqueous** solution, **water** can **compete**—predict **products** from **syllabus** **rules** and **relative** **potentials**.

---

**[Visual: Q = I × t then divide by Faraday constant]**

**Narrator:**  
**Charge** equals **current times time**. Divide by **Faraday’s constant** to get **moles of electrons**, then use the **half-equation** to find **moles of product** and **mass**.

---

**[Visual: Standard hydrogen electrode reference]**

**Narrator:**  
**Standard electrode potentials** rank **half-reactions**. **Subtract** appropriately to get **cell voltage**. A **positive** **standard cell potential** means **feasible** under **standard** conditions—**concentration** can still **shift** outcomes.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — electrolysis products, Faraday, E°cell]**

**Narrator:**  
Practise **writing** **half-equations** for **electrodes** and **balancing** **electrons**. Next: **enthalpy** and **Hess’s law**.
