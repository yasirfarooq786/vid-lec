## Electrolysis vs chemical cells

| Feature | **Electrolytic cell** | **Voltaic (chemical) cell** |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Energy** | **Electrical** energy drives **non-spontaneous** reaction | **Spontaneous** reaction produces **electrical** energy |
| **Anode** | **Positive** | **Negative** |
| **Cathode** | **Negative** | **Positive** |
| **Electrons in external wire** | From **negative** terminal of supply **to cathode**; from **anode** **to positive** terminal (reduction **gains** e⁻ at **cathode**) | From **anode** **to cathode** through the wire (**anode** is **negative** in a simple cell) |

**Mnemonic:** **AnOx** (**anode** **oxidation**), **RedCat** (**cathode** **reduction**) — **always** true.

---

## Electrolysis of molten salts

**Cations** **reduced** at **cathode**; **anions** **oxidised** at **anode**.  
**Example:** **NaCl (l)** → **Na** at **cathode**, **Cl₂** at **anode**.

---

## Electrolysis of aqueous solutions

**Competition:** **H⁺/OH⁻** from **water** may discharge depending on **concentration** and **relative** **E°**.  
**Dilute NaCl:** **H₂** and **O₂** often predominate; **concentrated NaCl:** **Cl₂** at **anode**.

---

## Faraday’s laws

**Q = I × t** (charge in **coulombs**).  
**Moles of electrons = Q / F** where **F ≈ 96500 C mol⁻¹**.

Use **stoichiometry** of **half-equation** to link **mass** of product to **charge** passed.

---

## Standard electrode potentials **E°**

**Half-cells** measured vs **standard hydrogen electrode** (**SHE**) at **298 K**, **1 bar**, **1 mol dm⁻³** **effective** concentration.

**E°cell = E°cathode(reduction) − E°anode(reduction)** for **cell** as written, **or** **E°right − E°left** for **conventional** **cell diagram** (follow **exam** convention).

**More positive** **E°** → **stronger** **oxidising** agent (reduced form **stable**); **more negative** → **stronger** **reducing** agent.

---

## Nernst equation (awareness)

**E** shifts with **concentration**; **Le Chatelier** reasoning for **qualitative** **changes**.

## Industrial electrolysis

- **Brine** → **Cl₂**, **H₂**, **NaOH** (**membrane** cell).  
- **Copper** **refining** (**impure** **anode**, **pure** **cathode**).

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Positive** **ion** goes to **negative** **cathode** in **electrolysis** (**opposites attract**).  
- **E°** predicts **direction** under **standard** conditions—**non-standard** may **reverse** **feasibility** if **Q** extreme.
