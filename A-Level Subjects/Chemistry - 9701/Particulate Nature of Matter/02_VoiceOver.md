# Voice-Over Script: Particulate Nature of Matter (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: Particle models — solid lattice, liquid jumble, gas sparse]**

**Narrator:**  
Chemistry treats matter as **particles**. In **solids** they **vibrate** in **fixed** positions. In **liquids** they **slide** past each other. In **gases** they are **far apart** and **move randomly**, **filling** the space.

---

**[Visual: Gas hitting walls — pressure as collisions]**

**Narrator:**  
The **kinetic model** pictures gas molecules as **tiny hard spheres** with **negligible** **volume** and **no attraction**, **colliding elastically**. **Pressure** comes from **hits** on the **container walls**. **Raising temperature** increases **average** kinetic energy.

---

**[Visual: pV = nRT with unit labels Pa, m³, mol, K]**

**Narrator:**  
The **ideal gas equation** links **pressure**, **volume**, **amount**, and **temperature**. Convert **dm³** to **cubic metres**, **kilopascals** to **pascals**, and **Celsius** to **kelvin** before you substitute. Check **conditions** for **molar volume** shortcuts.

---

**[Visual: Two Maxwell–Boltzmann curves — cold vs hot]**

**Narrator:**  
Molecules don’t all move at one speed. **Heating** **spreads** and **shifts** the **distribution**, so **more** particles have enough energy to **react**—you’ll connect that to **rates** later.
