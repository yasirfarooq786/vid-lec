## States of matter

| State | Particle arrangement | Particle motion | Shape / volume |
|-------|---------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Solid** | Regular **lattice**; close-packed | **Vibrate** about fixed positions | **Fixed** shape and volume |
| **Liquid** | Close but **disordered** | **Move** past one another | **Fixed** volume; takes **container** shape |
| **Gas** | **Far apart** (mostly empty space) | **Random**, **rapid** motion | **Fills** container; **compressible** |

**Changes of state:** melting, boiling, condensation, freezing, sublimation—involve **energy** changes without changing **chemical identity** (physical changes).

---

## Kinetic theory of gases (assumptions)

For an **ideal gas**, particles:

- Have **negligible volume** compared with container volume.
- **Exert no forces** on each other except during **collisions**.
- Are in **continuous random motion**; **collisions** are **perfectly elastic**.
- **Average kinetic energy** is **proportional** to **absolute temperature** (in kelvin).

**Real gases** deviate at **high pressure** / **low temperature** when **volume** of molecules and **intermolecular forces** matter.

---

## Ideal gas equation

**pV = nRT**

- **p** — pressure (Pa); **V** — volume (m³); **n** — amount (mol)
- **R** — molar gas constant (**8.31 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹**) when using SI units
- **T** — thermodynamic temperature (**K**) = **°C + 273.15**

**Calculations:** convert units consistently (e.g. dm³ → m³ × 10⁻³; kPa → Pa × 10³).

**Molar volume:** at **STP** (standard definitions vary—use **data booklet** / question), **Vm** ≈ **24 dm³ mol⁻¹** at **room temperature** and pressure in many **9701** questions (**confirm** stated conditions).

## Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution (concept)

At a given **T**, molecules have a **spread** of speeds; **curve** shifts **right** and **flattens** as **T** rises (**more** molecules exceed a given **activation energy**—links to **kinetics**).

---

## Liquids and simple solids

- **Melting / boiling points** reflect **strength** of **forces** between particles (**ionic**, **metallic**, **covalent** network vs **simple molecular**).
- **Evaporation** from a liquid: **faster** molecules escape → **cooling** (energy taken from liquid).

- Sketch **p–V**, **p–T**, **V–T** relationships for **fixed n** (ideal gas).
- Explain **pressure** as **force per unit area** from **particle collisions** with walls.
- Use **n = mass / Mr** and **pV = nRT** in **stoichiometry** gas problems.

## Common misconceptions

- **Heating** a gas in a **sealed rigid** container: **p** increases at **constant V**; **not** always “expansion.”
- **Ideal** gas is a **model**—**real** gases approximate it under **ordinary** conditions.
