## Brønsted–Lowry definitions

- **Acid:** **proton donor** (H⁺).  
- **Base:** **proton acceptor**.

**Conjugate pair:** **HA / A⁻**; **B / BH⁺**.

---

## Strong vs weak

| Type | Dissociation in water | **Ka** / **Kb** | pH example (0.1 mol dm⁻³) |
|------|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Strong acid** (HCl, HNO₃, H₂SO₄ first proton) | **Complete** | **Very large** | ≈ **1** |
| **Weak acid** (CH₃COOH) | **Partial** | **Small Ka** | **> 1** |
| **Strong base** (NaOH, KOH) | **Complete OH⁻** | — | ≈ **13** |
| **Weak base** (NH₃) | **Partial** | **Small Kb** | **< 13** |

**Ka × Kb = Kw** for **conjugate** pair in water at **given T** (**25 °C**: **Kw = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴**).

---

## pH and pKa

**pH = −log₁₀[H⁺]** (activities approximated by **concentration** in **9701** level).  
**pKa = −log₁₀ Ka** — **higher pKa** → **weaker** acid.

For **weak acid** (approximate): **[H⁺] ≈ √(Ka × ca)** when **dissociation** **small**.

---

## Salts

- **Strong acid + strong base** → **neutral** **salt** solution (e.g. **NaCl**).  
- **Weak acid + strong base** → **basic** **salt** (**CH₃COO⁻** **hydrolyses**).  
- **Strong acid + weak base** → **acidic** **salt** (**NH₄⁺** **hydrolyses**).

---

## Buffers

**Resist pH change** on **small** addition of **acid** or **base**.

**Acidic buffer:** **weak acid** + **conjugate salt** (e.g. **CH₃COOH / CH₃COO⁻**).  
**Henderson–Hasselbalch** (awareness): **pH = pKa + log([A⁻]/[HA])**.

**Basic buffer:** **weak base** + **salt** of its **conjugate acid** (e.g. **NH₃ / NH₄⁺**).

---

## Titration curves

- **Strong–strong:** **sharp** **vertical** **region** at **equivalence**; **pH 7** at **eq.**  
- **Weak acid–strong base:** **pH > 7** at **eq.** (**conjugate base**); **half-equivalence** **pH = pKa**.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Dilute** **strong** acid can have **higher pH** than **concentrated** **weak** acid—**compare** **[H⁺]**, not **labels**.  
- **Salt** solutions are **not** always **neutral**.
