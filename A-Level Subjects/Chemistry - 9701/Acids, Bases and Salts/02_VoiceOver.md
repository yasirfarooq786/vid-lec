# Voice-Over Script: Acids, Bases and Salts (Cambridge International AS & A Level Chemistry 9701)

**[Visual: HA donating H+ to water — arrow to H3O+]**

**Narrator:**  
**Brønsted–Lowry** acids **donate protons**; bases **accept** them. Every acid has a **conjugate base** left behind after **losing** **H plus**.

---

**[Visual: Strong acid 100% ions vs weak acid equilibrium]**

**Narrator:**  
**Strong** acids **fully ionise** in water—think **HCl** or **nitric acid**. **Weak** acids like **ethanoic** sit in **equilibrium** with a small **Ka**. **pH** is **minus log** of **hydrogen ion concentration**—usually in **moles per decimetre cubed**.

---

**[Visual: Salt in water — CH3COO- pulling H+ from water]**

**Narrator:**  
**Salts** can be **acidic** or **basic** in solution. **Acetate** from **sodium ethanoate** **accepts** protons from **water** **weakly**, releasing **hydroxide**—so the solution is **alkaline**. **Ammonium chloride** is **acidic** because **ammonium** is a **weak acid**.

---

**[Visual: Buffer beaker — add drop of acid, pH meter barely moves]**

**Narrator:**  
**Buffers** mix **weak acid** with its **salt** or **weak base** with its **ammonium salt**. They **soak up** added **H+** or **OH-** through **equilibrium shifts**. **Titration curves** show **buffer regions** as **flatter** **sections**.

---

**Narrator:**  
Practise **weak acid pH** estimates and **reading** **curves** for **equivalence** and **half-equivalence**. Next: **air** and **water** chemistry.
