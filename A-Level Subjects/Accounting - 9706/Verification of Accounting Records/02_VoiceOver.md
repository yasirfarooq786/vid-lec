# Voice-Over Script: Verification of Accounting Records (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

**[Visual: Bank statement beside cash book — unmatched lines highlighted]**

**Narrator:**  
**Bank reconciliation** explains **why** your **cash book** balance differs from the **bank statement**. First, **update** the cash book for items the **bank** already knows—**charges**, **interest**, **transfers**. Then **reconcile** timing items like **unpresented cheques** and **deposits not yet cleared**.

---

**[Visual: Sales ledger stack → equals → sales ledger control in general ledger]**

**Narrator:**  
**Control accounts** sit in the **general ledger** but **summarise** **all** **receivables** or **payables**. If the **control** balance **equals** the **sum** of **personal** accounts, your **ledgers** are **consistent**. If not, hunt **posting**, **casting**, or **transposition** errors.

---

**[Visual: Trial balance wobble → suspense account holds difference]**

**Narrator:**  
When debits and credits **don’t** match, you may open a **suspense account** as a **temporary** plug. **Find** each error, post **correcting journals**, and **clear** suspense to **zero**. Always state how errors **changed** **profit** if they hit **income** or **expenses**.

---

**[Visual: Two columns — “TB balances” vs “TB still balances but wrong profit”]**

**Narrator:**  
Some errors **don’t** upset the **trial balance**—for example **forgetting** a transaction **entirely**, or **compensating** mistakes. Those still **distort** the **financial statements**, so **verification** needs **control** checks and **review**, not only **totalling**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — bank rec, control accounts, suspense, error types]**

**Narrator:**  
Practise **full** **bank reconciliation** layouts and **control** **T-accounts** under **time pressure**. Next: **depreciation**, **accruals**, and **prepayments**.
