# Complete Lecture: Verification of Accounting Records (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

This lecture covers **bank reconciliation**, **control accounts**, the **suspense account**, and **types of errors**—how accountants **check** that records are **complete** and **consistent**. ## Why verification matters

Recording mistakes distort **profit**, **assets**, and **liabilities**. Verification procedures **detect** differences between **independent** records (e.g. **cash book** vs **bank statement**) and **cross-check** **subsidiary** ledgers against **general ledger** **control** totals.

---

## Bank reconciliation

### Causes of difference between cash book and bank statement

**Items in **cash book** but **not yet** on **statement** (timing):**

- **Unpresented cheques** (issued, not cleared).
- **Deposits not yet credited** (lodged, not on statement).

**Items on **statement** but **not** in **cash book** until **updated**:**

- **Bank charges**, **interest**, **direct debits**, **standing orders**, **credit transfers** received.
- **Errors** by bank or business (rare but examinable).

### Process (standard)

1. **Update** the **cash book** for **known** items (bank charges, interest, etc.).
2. **Reconcile** the **updated** cash book balance to the **statement** balance using a **reconciliation statement**:
   - Add/subtract **timing differences** (unpresented cheques, outstanding deposits).
   - Adjust for **bank errors** if any.

**Exam layout:** Show **adjusted cash book** **dr/cr** entries, then **statement** starting from **balance per bank statement**, **±** reconciling items → **balance per updated cash book**.

## Control accounts

### Sales ledger control account (total receivables)

Typically **kept** in **general ledger**; balance should equal **sum** of **individual** customer balances in **sales ledger**.

**Common entries (illustrative):**

- **DR** opening receivables, **credit sales** (from sales day book).
- **CR** cash/cheques received, **sales returns**, **bad debts**, **discount allowed**, **set-off** contra.

### Purchases ledger control account (total payables)

- **CR** opening payables, **credit purchases**.
- **DR** payments, **purchases returns**, **discount received**, **contra**.

### Uses

- **Prove** **arithmetical** accuracy of **ledgers** (if totals **don’t** match, **find** errors).
- **Extract** **trade receivables/payables** for **statement of financial position** quickly.

**Differences** may arise from **posting** to wrong ledger, **transposition**, **omission**, **casting** errors in day books.

---

## Suspense account

A **temporary** account used when the **trial balance** **does not balance** (or a **one-sided** error is discovered) until the **cause** is found.

- **One-sided** posting or **wrong** trial balance **extraction** often creates a **suspense** balance.
- When **correcting**, **journal** entries **clear** suspense to **nil** once **all** errors are fixed.

**Exam:** After listing **errors**, show **journal** dr/cr including **suspense**, then state **revised** profit if **P&L** items were wrong.

## Types of errors (summary)

| Effect on trial balance | Examples |
|-------------------------|----------|
| **Affects** trial balance (doesn’t balance) | **Single** entry, **different** amounts **dr** and **cr**, **wrong** side, **balance** **omitted** from TB |
| **Does not affect** trial balance | **Compensating** errors (equal **opposite** mistakes), **complete omission** of **both** entries, **original entry** correct **amount** but **wrong class** of account, **principle** errors |

### Error correction

- **Reverse** incorrect entry and **post** correct one, **or** use **one** **correcting** journal that **nets** the effect.
- Consider impact on **gross profit**, **profit for the year**, and **assets/liabilities**.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Bank reconciliation** does **not** replace updating the **cash book**—**permanent** items belong **in** the cash book.
- **Control account** **agreeing** with **sum** of **personal** accounts does **not** prove **every** individual balance is **right** if **two** customer accounts contain **offsetting** errors.
