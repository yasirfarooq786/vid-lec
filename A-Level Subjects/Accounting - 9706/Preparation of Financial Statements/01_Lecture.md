# Complete Lecture: Preparation of Financial Statements (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

This lecture focuses on **drafting** **financial statements** for **sole traders**, **partnerships**, and **limited companies**, including **manufacturing** and **incomplete records** where examined. ## Statement of profit or loss (and other comprehensive income)

**Purpose:** Measure **financial performance** over a **period** (usually one year).

### Typical layout — trading business (sole trader / partnership)

1. **Revenue** (sales **net** of returns).
2. **Less cost of sales:** opening inventory + purchases (+ carriage in) − purchases returns − closing inventory.
3. = **Gross profit**.
4. **Add** other income (e.g. **discount received**, **rent receivable**).
5. **Less** expenses (distribution, administration, depreciation, irrecoverable debts, etc.).
6. = **Profit for the year** (before **interest** and **tax** if shown separately).

**Partnership:** after **profit for the year**, prepare **appropriation account** (interest on capital, salaries, **residual** profit by **ratio**).

### Limited company (overview)

- May separate **cost of sales** and **operating expenses**; show **finance costs**, **tax**, **profit attributable** to owners.
- **Statement of changes in equity** tracks **share capital**, **retained earnings**, **reserves**.
- **Dividends** are **appropriations** of profit—**not** expenses in **P&L** (dividends **declared** reduce **retained earnings**).

## Statement of financial position

**Purpose:** **Financial position** at **one date**—assets, liabilities, **equity**.

### Order (common)

**Non-current assets** (cost, acc. dep., **carrying amount**)  
**Current assets** (inventory, receivables **net** of allowance, **prepayments**, cash)  
**Current liabilities** (payables, **bank overdraft**, accruals, **tax** payable)  
= **Net current assets / (liabilities)**  
**Non-current liabilities** (long-term loans)  
= **Net assets**  
**Financed by:** capital / **share capital** + **reserves** + **retained earnings** (and **current account** balances for partners).

**Capital section — sole trader:** opening capital + profit − drawings (± **capital introduced**).

## Manufacturing accounts (where required)

Separate **direct** costs (**materials**, **labour**) and **factory overheads** to find **production cost** of **finished goods**. **Cost** transfers to **trading account** as **cost of sales** when goods are **sold**.

---

## Incomplete records

When **no full** double entry exists:

- Use **accounting equation**, **mark-up** / **margin**, **control** **totals**, **cash** summaries, or **margin** on **cost** to **derive** **sales**, **purchases**, or **missing** **figures**.
- Build **cash book** **reconstruction** and **debtors/creditors** **working** accounts.

**Exam:** Show **clear** **workings**—marks follow **logical** steps.

---

## Partnership accounts (key points)

- **Appropriation** before **capital** / **current** **movements**.
- Each partner may have **fixed capital** + **current account** for **share** of profit, **drawings**, **interest**.

---

## Limited company — minimum awareness

- **Share capital** (nominal value × shares issued).
- **Reserves:** **retained earnings**, **share premium**, **revaluation** (if applicable).
- **Non-current liabilities:** **debentures** / loans.

*(Depth follows your **A Level** year and **past papers**.)*

## Presentation and labelling

- **Title** with **entity name** and **date** / **period**.
- **Comparative** figures if asked.
- **$** or **currency** with **thousand** separators as instructed.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Drawings** are **not** a **P&L** expense—they **reduce** **equity**.
- **Closing inventory** appears in **two** places in **workings** (SOFP **and** cost of sales)—**don’t** double-count in **trial balance** if already **adjusted**.
