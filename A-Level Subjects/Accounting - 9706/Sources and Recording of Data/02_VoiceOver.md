# Voice-Over Script: Sources and Recording of Data (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

**[Visual: Paper trail — invoice → day book → ledger]**

**Narrator:**  
Before you prepare **financial statements**, you must **record** transactions reliably. **Source documents** like **invoices** and **credit notes** prove what happened. Data flows into **books of prime entry**, then into **ledgers**.

---

**[Visual: T-accounts — “Debit left, Credit right” mnemonic for A/L/C/I/E]**

**Narrator:**  
**Double entry** means every transaction has **two** equal sides. **Assets** and **expenses** normally **increase** on the **debit** side. **Liabilities**, **capital**, and **income** normally **increase** on the **credit** side. Practise until this is **automatic**.

---

**[Visual: Row of day books — sales, purchases, returns, cash, journal]**

**Narrator:**  
The **sales day book** lists **credit sales**; totals post to **receivables** and **sales**. **Purchases** and **returns** books mirror that logic for **suppliers**. The **cash book** captures **bank** movements and often sits in the **trial balance** as the **bank account**. The **journal** handles **non-routine** entries.

---

**[Visual: Three ledgers — general, sales, purchases]**

**Narrator:**  
The **general ledger** holds **nominal** accounts. The **sales ledger** is **per customer**; the **purchases ledger** is **per supplier**. Together they support **control account** checks in the next topic.

---

**[Visual: Trial balance listing — totals must agree]**

**Narrator:**  
A **trial balance** lists **balances**. If **total debits** equal **total credits**, the **books** are **arithmetically** balanced—but **errors** can still hide. Never confuse “**balances**” with “**correct**”.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — documents, day books, ledger, trial balance]**

**Narrator:**  
Drill **posting** from **day books** to **ledgers** and build **speed** on **journal entries**. Next: **bank reconciliation** and **control accounts**.
