# Complete Lecture: Sources and Recording of Data (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

This lecture covers **source documents**, **books of prime entry**, **double-entry bookkeeping**, **ledgers**, and the **trial balance**—the **recording** foundation for all financial statements. Programme: [Cambridge 9706](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-accounting-9706/).

## Source documents

These **evidence** transactions and support **audit trails**:

| Document | Typical use |
|----------|-------------|
| **Invoice** (sales / purchase) | **Credit** sale or purchase |
| **Credit note** | **Returns** or **allowances** |
| **Receipt / petty cash voucher** | **Cash** movements |
| **Bank statement** | **Bank** transactions |
| **Payroll** records | **Wages** and **salaries** |

**Exam tip:** State **why** the document exists (e.g. credit note reduces amount owed after **returns**).

---

## Double-entry concept

Every transaction has **two equal effects** on the accounting equation:

- **Debit** entries and **credit** entries **must balance** for each transaction (and in total).

### T-account rules (assets, liabilities, capital, income, expenses)

**Balances (standard approach used in 9706 teaching):**

| Account type | **Increase** | **Decrease** |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Assets** | **Debit** | **Credit** |
| **Liabilities** | **Credit** | **Debit** |
| **Capital / equity** | **Credit** | **Debit** |
| **Income / revenue** | **Credit** | **Debit** |
| **Expenses** | **Debit** | **Credit** |

**Profit** links to equity: **income minus expenses** increases **capital** (via **profit for the year**).

## Books of prime entry (journals)

Transactions are **first** recorded in **day books** (summarised), then **posted** to the **ledger**.

| Book | Records |
|-------|---------|
| **Sales day book** | **Credit sales** (listed by invoice); total posted to **DR receivables**, **CR sales** |
| **Purchases day book** | **Credit purchases**; **DR purchases** (or expense), **CR payables** |
| **Sales returns day book** | **DR sales returns** (or reduce sales), **CR receivables** |
| **Purchases returns day book** | **DR payables**, **CR purchases returns** |
| **Cash book** | **All bank** (and often **cash**) receipts and payments—may be **multi-column** |
| **Petty cash book** | Small **imprest** payments |
| **General journal** | **Opening entries**, **corrections**, **depreciation**, **transfers**, **non-routine** items |

**Cash book** often doubles as a **ledger account** for **bank** in the trial balance.

---

## Ledgers

- **Sales ledger** (receivables ledger): **each customer** account—**DR** balance = amount **owed** to the business.
- **Purchases ledger** (payables ledger): **each supplier** account—**CR** balance = amount **owed by** the business.
- **General ledger:** all **nominal** accounts—expenses, income, assets, liabilities, capital, **provision** accounts, etc.

---

## Trial balance

A **list** of **every ledger account** with its **balance** (debit or credit).

**Purposes:**

1. **Check** that total **debits** = total **credits** (arithmetic **integrity** of posting).
2. **Starting point** for **year-end adjustments** and **financial statements**.

**Limitations:** a trial balance can **balance** yet still contain **errors** (e.g. **compensating** errors, **complete omission** of a transaction, **wrong amount** on **both** sides). **Topic 3** deals with **detection** and **correction**.

---

## Discounts (common in exam questions)

- **Trade discounts:** deducted **before** invoice total—**not** shown separately in ledger (sale recorded **net**).
- **Cash / settlement discounts:** for **early payment**; **settlement** may be recorded using methods your specification teaches (learn **exact** **dr/cr** pattern your past papers use).

---

## Inventory and cost of sales (recording link)

- **Purchases** accumulate cost of goods **bought**; **year-end** **inventory** valuation adjusts **cost of sales** in financial statements (**Topic 5**).

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Sales day book** does **not** replace the **sales ledger**—it **feeds** it.
- **Trial balance** proves **double-entry balance**, **not** that every figure is **correct**.
