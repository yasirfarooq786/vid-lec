# Complete Lecture: Accounting Procedures (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

This lecture covers **year-end procedures** that turn **raw ledger balances** into **fair** financial statements: **capital and revenue expenditure**, **depreciation**, **disposal** of non-current assets, **irrecoverable debts** and **allowances**, **accruals** and **prepayments**, and related **journal** work. Programme: [Cambridge 9706](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-accounting-9706/).

## Capital vs revenue expenditure

| Type | Examples | Treatment |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Capital expenditure** | Purchase of **non-current asset**, **legal fees** on **purchase**, **installation** | **Capitalise**—becomes **non-current asset** (may be **depreciated**) |
| **Revenue expenditure** | **Repairs**, **maintenance**, **fuel**, **wages** | **Expense** in **statement of profit or loss** in the **period** |

**Incorrect classification** misstates **profit** and **assets**.

## Depreciation

**Depreciation** allocates the **depreciable amount** (cost − **residual value**) of a **non-current asset** over its **useful life**—matching **economic benefit** to **periods**.

### Methods (9706 core)

**Straight-line (fixed instalment):**

**(Cost − Residual value) ÷ Useful life (years)** = **annual charge**  
(or **given** as **%** of **cost**).

**Reducing balance:**

**Carrying amount × Rate%** each year—**higher** charge **early** in life; **residual** often handled by **stopping** near end or adjusting **rate**.

### Ledger entries (typical)

- **DR** depreciation expense (P&L)  
- **CR** accumulated depreciation (**contra** to **asset** on SOFP)

**Carrying amount** = **Cost** − **Accumulated depreciation**.

### Disposal of non-current assets

1. **Remove** **cost** (**DR** disposal account; **CR** asset).
2. **Remove** **accumulated depreciation** (**DR** acc. dep.; **CR** disposal).
3. Record **proceeds** (**DR** cash / receivables; **CR** disposal).
4. **Balance** on **disposal account** = **profit** or **loss** on disposal → transfer to **P&L**.

## Irrecoverable debts and allowances

### Irrecoverable debt (bad debt)

Customer **won’t** pay; **write off**:

- **DR** irrecoverable debts expense  
- **CR** trade receivables

(if **already** in **allowance** situation, follow **exam** method given)

### Allowance for doubtful debts

**Prudent** estimate of **receivables** unlikely to collect—**not** specific known debts yet (or **combination** approach per syllabus).

- **DR** doubtful debts expense (increase in allowance)  
- **CR** allowance for doubtful debts (**deduction** from **receivables** on SOFP)

**Change** in allowance from **prior year** hits **P&L** as **increase** or **decrease** in **expense**.

## Accruals and prepayments

### Accruals (amounts **owed** / **incurred** but **not** paid or **recorded**)

- **Accrued expenses:** **DR** expense, **CR** accrued liabilities (payables / accruals).
- **Accrued income:** **DR** accrued income (receivable), **CR** income.

### Prepayments (amounts **paid** in **advance**)

- **Prepaid expenses:** **DR** prepayment (**asset**), **CR** expense (reduce **charge** to **P&L**).
- **Income received in advance (deferred income):** **DR** income, **CR** deferred income (**liability**).

**Exam:** Adjust **trial balance** figures to **accrual basis** before **drafting** statements.

## Other common adjustments

- **Closing inventory:** **DR** inventory (**SOFP**), **CR** **purchases** / **cost of sales** (method per your course).
- **Goods for own use:** **DR** drawings / **purchases**, **CR** purchases (owner takes inventory).

---

## Impact on financial statements

Each adjustment changes **profit**, **assets**, **liabilities**, or **capital**. Always ask: **P&L** or **SOFP**? **One** or **both**?

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Depreciation** is **not** a **cash** flow—**no** **bank** entry when **charging** depreciation.
- **Allowance** is a **contra asset**, **not** a **bank** account.
- **Accrual** vs **prepayment**: accrual = **owe** more expense; prepayment = **paid** **too early**.
