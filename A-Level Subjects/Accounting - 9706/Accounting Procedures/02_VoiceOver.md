# Voice-Over Script: Accounting Procedures (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

**[Visual: “Capital vs revenue” — machine purchase vs repair bill]**

**Narrator:**  
**Capital expenditure** buys or improves **long-term** assets. **Revenue expenditure** is day-to-day **running** costs. Mix them up and you **distort** both **profit** and **asset** values.

---

**[Visual: Straight-line bar chart vs reducing-balance declining curve]**

**Narrator:**  
**Depreciation** spreads **cost** over **useful life**. **Straight line** charges **equal** amounts. **Reducing balance** charges a **percentage** of **carrying amount**, so expense **falls** each year. Both use **accumulated depreciation** as a **contra** asset on the **statement of financial position**.

---

**[Visual: Disposal T-account — cost out, depreciation out, proceeds in, profit/loss]**

**Narrator:**  
When you **sell** an asset, open a **disposal account**. Strip out **cost** and **accumulated depreciation**, record **proceeds**, and whatever **remains** is **gain** or **loss** on disposal for the **profit or loss** statement.

---

**[Visual: Customer invoice crossed out — bad debt journal]**

**Narrator:**  
**Irrecoverable debts** remove **hopeless** balances from **receivables**. An **allowance for doubtful debts** is a **prudent** **estimate** for **doubtful** amounts still **outstanding**. The **movement** in the allowance hits **profit**.

---

**[Visual: Calendar — “accrued = owed”; “prepaid = paid early”]**

**Narrator:**  
**Accruals** add **expense** or **income** **earned** but **not yet** swapped for **cash** entries. **Prepayments** **defer** expense or **income** to the **next** period. These adjustments **implement** **accrual accounting**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — depreciation, disposal, debts, accruals/prepayments]**

**Narrator:**  
Drill **journals** until each adjustment **automatically** maps to **P&L** versus **SOFP**. Next: **full** **financial statements**.
