# Voice-Over Script: The Purpose of Accounting (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

**[Visual: Title — “9706: The purpose of accounting” — icons: owner, bank, government]**

**Narrator:**  
Accounting is how businesses **capture** and **communicate** financial information. In **9706** you focus mainly on **financial accounting**: building **financial statements** that **owners**, **lenders**, **tax authorities**, and other **stakeholders** can trust and use.

---

**[Visual: Split — “Financial vs management accounting”]**

**Narrator:**  
**Financial accounting** follows **consistent rules** and produces **formal reports**—often **annual**. **Management accounting** supports **internal** decisions like **pricing** and **budgets**; it is **flexible** and usually **private**.

---

**[Visual: Stakeholder wheel — each linked to a question]**

**Narrator:**  
Different **users** ask different questions. **Owners** care about **profit** and **return**. **Banks** care about **repayment**. **Suppliers** watch **liquidity**. **Government** cares about **tax** and **compliance**. Good answers come from **statements** and **ratios** you learn later.

---

**[Visual: Three business types — sole trader, partnership, company]**

**Narrator:**  
A **sole trader** has **one owner** and usually **unlimited liability**. A **partnership** shares ownership and splits profit by an **agreement**. A **limited company** is a **separate legal person** with **limited liability** for shareholders and **stricter** reporting rules.

---

**[Visual: Scales — Assets on one side; Capital + Liabilities on the other]**

**Narrator:**  
The **accounting equation** says **assets** equal **capital** plus **liabilities**. Every **transaction** keeps that balance. **Profit** grows **equity**; **drawings** shrink it. This idea underpins **double entry** in the next topic.
