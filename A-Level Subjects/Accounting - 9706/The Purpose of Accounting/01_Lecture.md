# Complete Lecture: The Purpose of Accounting (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

This lecture introduces **why** accounting exists, **who** uses financial information, and **how** the subject is organised in the **9706** specification. Official programme: [Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting (9706)](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-accounting-9706/).

## What is accounting?

**Accounting** is the **process of identifying, measuring, recording, classifying, summarising, and communicating** financial information so that **decisions** can be made and **performance** can be assessed.

It is **not** simply “doing sums”—it is a **disciplined language** for reporting **financial position** (what the business **owns** and **owes**) and **financial performance** (whether it made a **profit** or **loss** over a period).

---

## Main branches (overview)

| Branch | Primary users | Focus |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Financial accounting** | **External** users (owners, lenders, tax authorities, regulators) | **Published** financial statements following **rules** and **policies** |
| **Management accounting** | **Internal** managers | **Budgets**, **costs**, **forecasts**—often **not** published |

**9706** concentrates on **financial accounting** for **sole traders**, **partnerships**, and **limited companies**, with **analysis** of published information.

---

## Users of accounting information and their needs

- **Owners / shareholders:** profit, return, risk, dividends, growth.
- **Managers:** detailed results, control, planning (overlap with management accounting).
- **Lenders / creditors:** ability to **repay**; **security** of lending.
- **Suppliers:** **liquidity**—will they be paid?
- **Customers:** **continuity** of supply (especially long-term contracts).
- **Employees:** **stability**, profitability affecting **jobs** and **wages**.
- **Government / tax authorities:** **taxable profit**, compliance.
- **Investors (potential):** **risk** and **return** before buying shares.

**Exam angle:** Be able to **match** a **user** to the **type of decision** and **which statement** or **ratio** they would emphasise.

## Types of business organisation (9706 core)

### Sole trader

- **One owner**; **unlimited liability** (personal assets at risk if the business cannot pay debts).
- **Capital** introduced by the owner; **drawings** reduce equity.
- Financial statements: **statement of profit or loss**, **statement of financial position**, sometimes **statement of changes in equity** style reconciliation of capital.

### Partnership

- **Two or more** owners; partnership agreement covers **profit-sharing ratios**, **interest on capital**, **salaries** to partners, etc.
- Usually **unlimited liability** (general partners)—**limited liability partnerships** exist in some jurisdictions; use **syllabus** examples only.
- **Appropriation account** shows how **profit** is shared.

### Limited company

- **Separate legal personality**; **limited liability** for shareholders (to the amount unpaid on shares).
- **Share capital**, **reserves**, **directors**, **dividends**; **regulation** and **disclosure** stronger than for unincorporated businesses.
- **Published** accounts for **external** users; topics developed later in the course (A Level depth).

## The accounting equation

Every transaction maintains:

**Assets = Capital + Liabilities**  
(or **Assets = Equity + Liabilities**, where **equity** is **capital** plus **profits** minus **drawings** for a sole trader).

- **Assets:** resources controlled by the business (inventory, receivables, cash, non-current assets).
- **Liabilities:** obligations to pay (payables, loans, accrued expenses).
- **Capital / equity:** **residual interest**—what is **left** for the owner(s) after liabilities.

**Profit** increases **equity**; **drawings** / **dividends** reduce it.

---

## Stewardship and accountability

Owners often **delegate** running the business to **managers**. **Accounting records** and **financial statements** show whether **stewards** have used resources **properly** and report **results honestly**.

## Qualitative characteristics (link to Topic 6)

You will refine this later: information should be **relevant** and **faithfully represented** (complete, neutral, free from error), **comparable**, **verifiable**, **timely**, and **understandable**. **Materiality** and **cost** constrain how much detail is provided.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Profit** is **not** the same as **cash**—**accrual accounting** recognises income and expenses when **earned/incurred**, not only when **cash moves**.
- **Book value** of an asset is **not** necessarily **market value**.
