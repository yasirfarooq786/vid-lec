## Concepts and conventions (framework ideas)

### Going concern

Financial statements assume the business will **continue operating** for the **foreseeable future** (not **forced** into **liquidation**). Affects **valuation** (e.g. **non-current assets** **depreciated** over **use**, not **forced-sale** values).

### Accruals basis (matching)

**Income** and **expenses** are recognised when **earned** or **incurred**, **not** only when **cash** moves. Links **revenue** to **costs** of **earning** it in the **same period**.

### Consistency

Same **accounting policies** from **period** to **period** unless **change** is **justified** and **disclosed**—allows **meaningful** **comparison**.

### Prudence (careful application)

**Do not overstate** assets or income; **do not understate** liabilities or expenses. Historically linked to **provisions** and **inventory** at **lower of cost and NRV**. (Modern frameworks also stress **neutrality**—**prudence** must **not** create **hidden reserves**.)

### Materiality

**Omissions** or **misstatements** that could **influence decisions** must be **corrected**; **trivial** items may be **grouped** or **simplified**—**cost** vs **benefit**.

### Money measurement

Only items **measurable in money** appear in accounts—excludes some **intangibles** unless **recognised** under **rules** (e.g. purchased **goodwill**).

### Business entity

Financial statements report the **business** as **separate** from **owners**—**drawings** are **not** **business expenses**.

### Duality

Every transaction has **two** effects—basis of **double entry**.

### Historical cost

Assets often recorded at **original cost** (minus **depreciation** / **amortisation**). **Revaluation** allowed for some items under **standards** (A Level depth if examined).

## Accounting policies

**Policies** are **specific** methods chosen to apply concepts: e.g. **straight-line** vs **reducing balance** depreciation, **FIFO** vs **AVCO** for inventory (if syllabus includes), **recognition** of **revenue**.

**Change in policy** generally **retrospective** adjustment in **full** IFRS context; **9706** questions may ask for **effect on profit** or **reason** for **change**.

---

## True and fair view / fair presentation

Statements should **faithfully represent** the **economic reality** of **transactions** within the **framework**—**not** **misleading**.

---

## International standards (awareness)

**9706** may reference **IAS/IFRS** themes: **inventory** (IAS 2), **property, plant and equipment** (IAS 16), **revenue** (IFRS 15) ideas at **principle** level—**measurement**, **recognition**, **disclosure**. Use **syllabus** and **past papers** for **required depth**; **do not** import **professional exam** detail beyond specification.

## Ethics and professional behaviour (synoptic)

- **Integrity**, **objectivity**, **professional competence**, **confidentiality**, **professional behaviour**.
- **Window dressing** (e.g. **temporarily** repaying loans **before** year-end **only** to **improve** SOFP) is **misleading**.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Prudence** does **not** mean **always** choose the **worst** number—it must stay **consistent** with **faithful representation**.
- **Policies** are **not** the same as **estimates**—**useful life** of an asset is an **estimate**; **depreciation method** is a **policy**.
