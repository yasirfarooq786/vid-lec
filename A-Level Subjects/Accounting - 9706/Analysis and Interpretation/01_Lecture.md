## Why analyse accounts?

**Users** compare **performance** and **risk** across **time** and **competitors**. Ratios **standardise** figures (e.g. **profit** relative to **sales** or **capital**) so **different-sized** businesses can be **compared**—**with care**.

---

## Profitability ratios

| Ratio (typical names) | Numerator / denominator (check your syllabus) | Meaning |
|------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|---------|
| **Gross profit margin** | Gross profit ÷ Revenue × 100% | **Pricing** / **cost of sales** control |
| **Profit margin** (net) | Profit for year ÷ Revenue × 100% | **Overall** profitability after **all** expenses |
| **Return on capital employed (ROCE)** | Profit (often PBIT) ÷ Capital employed × 100% | Efficiency of **long-term** **funds** |
| **Return on equity (ROE)** | Profit for year ÷ Equity × 100% | Return to **owners** |

**Capital employed** may be **equity + non-current liabilities** or **non-current assets + net current assets**—**use the definition** in the **question**.

---

## Liquidity ratios

| Ratio | Formula area | Meaning |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Current ratio** | Current assets ÷ Current liabilities | Short-term **solvency** buffer |
| **Quick (acid test) ratio** | (Current assets − Inventory) ÷ Current liabilities | Liquidity **without** relying on **inventory** sales |

**Too high** may mean **idle** cash; **too low** risks **insolvency**.

---

## Efficiency (activity) ratios

| Ratio | Idea | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Inventory turnover** | Cost of sales ÷ Average inventory | How fast **inventory** **sells** |
| **Trade receivables turnover / collection period** | Receivables × days ÷ Credit sales | **Credit control** |
| **Trade payables payment period** | Payables × days ÷ Credit purchases (or COS) | **Supplier** **terms** |

**Always** match **period** (days in year **365** unless told otherwise).

---

## Gearing (risk)

| Ratio | Idea | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Gearing** (various) | Debt ÷ (Debt + Equity) or Debt ÷ Equity | **Proportion** of **borrowed** funds—**higher** → **more risk** but **potential** **leverage** |

Interest cover: PBIT ÷ Finance costs—ability to **pay interest**.

## Interpretation skills (examination)

1. **State the trend** (increase/decrease) with **numbers**.
2. **Suggest cause** linked to **business** (e.g. **discounting** reduces **margin**).
3. **Consider** **liquidity** vs **profitability** **trade-offs** (e.g. **loose** credit boosts **sales** but **ties** up **cash**).
4. **Limitations:** **historical** cost, **one-off** items, **different** **accounting policies**, **seasonality**, **lack of** **non-financial** data.

---

## Common misconceptions

- **Higher** sales with **lower** margin may yield **less** **absolute** **profit**—**always** check **both**.
- **ROCE** and **profit margin** measure **different** things—**do not** **conflate**.
