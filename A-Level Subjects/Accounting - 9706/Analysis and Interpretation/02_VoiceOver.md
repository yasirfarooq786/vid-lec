# Voice-Over Script: Analysis and Interpretation (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

**[Visual: Dashboard of ratios — profitability, liquidity, efficiency]**

**Narrator:**  
**Analysis** turns **financial statements** into **decisions**. **Ratios** rescale numbers so you can see **margins**, **returns**, **liquidity**, and **how fast** **working capital** **turns**.

---

**[Visual: Gross profit % and net profit % on same chart]**

**Narrator:**  
**Gross profit margin** tracks **buying** and **pricing**. **Net profit margin** adds **overheads** and **finance**. **ROCE** asks whether **long-term** **resources** earn enough **return**. Always use the **exact** **profit** figure your **formula** requires—**PBIT** vs **profit before tax**, for example.

---

**[Visual: Current vs quick ratio — inventory greyed out in quick]**

**Narrator:**  
**Liquidity** ratios test **short-term** **survival**. The **quick** ratio **excludes** **inventory** because it may **not** sell **quickly**. **Interpret** **too high** as well as **too low**.

---

**[Visual: Receivables days — calendar animation]**

**Narrator:**  
**Efficiency** ratios link **working capital** to **activity**. **Collection** **period** and **inventory** **turnover** show **tight** or **slack** **management**. Link them to **cash** **strain** in your **written** answers.

---

**[Visual: Gearing lever — debt vs equity]**

**Narrator:**  
**Gearing** shows **dependence** on **borrowed** money. **Higher** gearing **amplifies** **returns** in good times but **raises** **risk** when **profits** or **interest rates** **move** the wrong way. Mention **interest cover** when **debt** is **large**.

---

**[Visual: “Limitations” sticky notes — historical cost, policies differ]**

**Narrator:**  
Always **qualify** analysis: **past** figures, **policy** **changes**, **one-off** **items**, and **missing** **context** like **market** **conditions** or **quality** of **management**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — calculate, trend, explain, limit]**

**Narrator:**  
Exam answers need **numbers**, **trend**, **because**, and **so what** for the **business**. Next: **past paper** **technique**.
