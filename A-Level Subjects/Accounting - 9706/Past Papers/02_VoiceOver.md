# Voice-Over Script: Using Past Papers (Cambridge International AS & A Level Accounting 9706)

**[Visual: 9706 papers 1–3 icons]**

**Narrator:**  
**Paper 1** trains **speed** and **accuracy** on **multiple choice**. **Papers 2 and 3** are **written**—they reward **correct layouts**, **complete workings**, and **clear** **explanations**. Always confirm **your** **exam series** **structure** on the **cover** page.

---

**[Visual: Highlighter on “Prepare” and “Explain”]**

**Narrator:**  
When a question says **prepare**, produce the **full** **statement** or **account**. When it says **explain**, add **reasoning**—for example **how** **depreciation** **matches** **accruals** or **why** **gearing** **increases** **risk**.

---

**[Visual: Workings column — “method marks”]**

**Narrator:**  
**Show every working**. Many marks use **own figure rule**: if you make an **early** slip but your **method** stays **logical**, you still **carry** **forward**. **No workings** means **no** **evidence**.

---

**[Visual: Timer split by question marks]**

**Narrator:**  
Plan **minutes** using **marks**. **Bank reconciliations** and **control accounts** eat **time**—practice until they are **routine**. Leave a **few** minutes to **check** **balances** and **labels**.

---

**[Visual: Error log notebook]**

**Narrator:**  
After each paper, **log** mistakes: **disposal** accounts, **allowance** **increase** vs **balance**, **accruals** **direction**. **Redo** those **types** until they **stick**.

---

**[Visual: Checklist — timed paper, mark scheme, error log, repeat]**

**Narrator:**  
**Past papers** are the **best** **revision** when used **actively**: **attempt**, **mark**, **fix**, **repeat**. Good luck—and **show** your **workings**.
