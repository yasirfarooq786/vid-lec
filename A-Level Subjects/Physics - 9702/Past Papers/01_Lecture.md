# Complete Lecture: Past Papers and Examination Technique (Cambridge International AS & A Level Physics 9702)

This lecture is **not** a syllabus topic—it is a **structured** approach to **9702** **past papers**, **mark schemes**, and **practical** **skills** so you **convert** **knowledge** into **marks**. Programme: [Cambridge 9702](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-physics-9702/).

---

## 1. What past papers train

- **Command words** (**state**, **explain**, **derive**, **show that**, **suggest**, **compare**).  
- **SI units**, **significant figures**, and **scientific notation** under **time** **pressure**.  
- **Layout** that **examiners** **can** **follow** (**method** **marks**).

---

## 2. Before you start a question

1. **Read** the **final** **prompt** first—**know** the **target** **quantity**.  
2. **Underline** **given** **data** and **hidden** **assumptions** (**smooth**, **light string**, **ideal** **gas**, **uniform** **field**).  
3. **Choose** **diagrams** **where** they **replace** **paragraphs** of **text**.

---

## 3. Calculations that score

- **Write** **equation** **in** **symbols** **before** **numbers**.  
- **Rearrange** **then** **substitute** ( **reduces** **algebra** **errors** ).  
- **Carry** **one** **extra** **sig** **fig** **internally**; **round** **final** **answer** to **match** **weakest** **datum** unless **question** **specifies**.  
- **Unit** **check** **dimensions** **mentally** for **obvious** **power-of-ten** **mistakes**.

---

## 4. Definitions and explanations

- **Definition** **questions** need **both** **sides** of the **ratio** or **the** **full** **law** (**e.g.** **field** **strength** **as** **force per unit** …).  
- **Explain** **means** **physics** **mechanism** + **link** to **observation**—**not** **storytelling**.

---

## 5. Graphs and practical papers

- **Axes** **labels** with **units**; **even** **scales**; **plots** **small** **crosses**; **line** of **best** **fit** **ignores** **outliers** **when** **justified**.  
- **Gradient** / **intercept** **with** **triangle** **large** **enough** on **graph** **questions**.  
- **Uncertainty** **language**—**precision** vs **accuracy**; **repeat** **readings** **mean** **random** **error** **reduction**.

---

## 6. Using mark schemes effectively

- **Compare** your **terminology** to **MS** **keywords** after **timed** **attempt**—**then** **rewrite** **weak** **sections** **from** **memory**.  
- **Track** **error** **types** (**signs**, **vectors**, **radians** **vs** **degrees**, **missing** **factor** **½**).
