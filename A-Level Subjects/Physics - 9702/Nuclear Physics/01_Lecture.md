# Complete Lecture: Nuclear Physics (Cambridge International AS & A Level Physics 9702)

This lecture covers **the nucleus**, **radioactivity**, **decay laws**, **binding energy**, and **nuclear reactions** for **9702**. Programme: [Cambridge 9702](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-physics-9702/).

## 1. Structure of the atom and nucleus

**Nucleus:** **protons** (**charge +e**) + **neutrons** (**neutral**). **Nuclide** notation **ᴬ_Z X** (**A** mass **number**, **Z** atomic **number**).

**Isotopes:** **same Z**, **different A** (different **neutron** **count**).

**Strong nuclear force:** **short** **range**, **binds** **nucleons**; **overcomes** **Coulomb** **repulsion** in **stable** **nuclei** (with **neutron**/**proton** **balance**).

---

## 2. Radioactivity

**Random** **decay** of **individual** **nuclei**; **predictable** **statistics** for **large** **samples**.

**Alpha (α):** **He nucleus** **⁴₂α** — **high** **ionisation**, **low** **penetration** in **matter**.

**Beta-minus (β⁻):** **electron** **emitted**; **neutron** → **proton** + **e⁻** + **ν̄_e** ( **A** **unchanged**, **Z** **increases** **by** **1**).

**Beta-plus / electron capture** (if **in** **syllabus** **depth**): **Z** **decreases**.

**Gamma (γ):** **electromagnetic** **photon** from **de-excited** **nucleus** — **no** **change** in **A** or **Z**.

**Conservation** in **equations:** **charge**, **mass** **number**, **lepton** **number** (as **required**).

---

## 3. Decay constant and half-life

**Activity A** (**becquerel** **Bq** = **decays s⁻¹**): **A = λN** (**λ** **decay** **constant**, **N** **number** of **unstable** **nuclei**).

**Exponential** **decay:** **N = N₀ e^(−λt)**, **A = A₀ e^(−λt)**.

**Half-life T½:** **time** for **N** **or** **A** **to** **halve**; **λ = ln 2 / T½**.

---

## 4. Mass–energy and binding energy

**Mass defect:** **difference** between **sum** of **nucleon** **masses** and **nuclear** **mass**.

**Binding energy:** **energy** **released** forming **nucleus** from **separated** **nucleons**; **binding energy per nucleon** **curve** — **peak** near **iron** **region** (**qualitative** **importance**).

**E = mc²** for **energy** **releases** in **reactions** (**mass** **decrease** **→** **energy** **products**).

---

## 5. Fission and fusion

**Induced** **fission** (e.g. **²³⁵U** + **neutron**) — **chain** **reactions**, **moderators**, **control** **rods**.

**Fusion** — **powers** **stars**; **high** **activation** **energy** / **temperature** **needed** on **Earth** for **practical** **devices**.

---

## 6. Radiation safety and detection

**Ionisation** **damage**; **shielding** **depends** on **radiation** **type**.

**Detectors** ( **GM** **tube**, **cloud** **chamber** **principles** ) — **syllabus** **applications**.

## Common misconceptions

- **Half-life** **does not** **mean** **“all** **gone** **after** **two** **half-lives**” — **exponential**, **not** **linear**.  
- **β** **particles** **are not** **orbital** **electrons** **from** **shell** in **β⁻** **decay** (they’re **created** in **weak** **interaction** **at** **A** **Level** **treat** **as** **given** **process**).  
- **γ** **does** **not** **change** **Z** or **A** — **nucleus** **already** **rearranged**.
