# Complete Lecture: Evaluation and Judgement (Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives 0457)

Programme: [Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives (0457)](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-igcse-global-perspectives-0457/).

---

## 1. Criteria for judgement

- Choose **2–4 criteria** (effectiveness, fairness, cost, sustainability, feasibility) and apply them **consistently**.

---

## 2. Strengths and limitations

- For each policy or proposal: **what it achieves**, **what it misses**, **who is left out**.

---

## 3. Balanced conclusions

- “**To what extent**” answers need a **verdict** tied to evidence weight—not a 50/50 waffle.

---

## Common misconceptions

- New evidence in the **final line**—integrate earlier; conclusion **summarises** judgement.

---

## Revision checklist

- [ ] Write a **criteria table** and score two options 1–5 with one-line justifications.
- [ ] End one essay with a **one-sentence** judgement referencing criteria.
- [ ] Peer-review a partner’s evaluation for **hidden assumptions**.
