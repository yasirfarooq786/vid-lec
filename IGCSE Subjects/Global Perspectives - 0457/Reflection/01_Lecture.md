# Complete Lecture: Reflection (Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives 0457)

This material supports **Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives (0457)**—skills for enquiry into global issues, structured argument, and assessed components. Programme: [Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives (0457)](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-igcse-global-perspectives-0457/).

---

## 1. What reflection adds

- Shows how your thinking **changed** with new evidence or collaboration.
- Links **personal position** to **fair treatment** of other views.

---

## 2. Useful prompts

- What surprised me? What challenged my assumption? What would I research next?

---

## 3. For Component 2 especially

- Reflection is often **assessed** as structured writing—avoid diary drift; keep **academic tone**.

---

## Common misconceptions

- “Reflection = feelings only.” Feelings may appear, but **learning** and **reasons** must lead.

---

## Revision checklist

- [ ] Write **300 words** answering the three prompts above for one project cycle.
- [ ] Identify **one** bias you caught in yourself and how you corrected it.
- [ ] Link reflection to **specific** evidence you read.
