# Complete Lecture: Research Skills and Evidence (Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives 0457)

This material supports **Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives (0457)**—skills for enquiry into global issues, structured argument, and assessed components. Programme: [Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives (0457)](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-igcse-global-perspectives-0457/).

---

## 1. Planning enquiry

- Turn a broad topic into a **focused question** with clear **scope** (time, place, group).

---

## 2. Sources and triangulation

- Prefer **independent** verification: compare **official data**, **academic**, and **reputable journalism** where possible.
- Note **date**, **author**, **publisher**, and **method** (survey size, sampling limits).

---

## 3. Referencing and integrity

- Paraphrase with understanding; **cite** direct quotations; avoid **cherry-picking** out of context.

---

## Common misconceptions

- “More sources = better.” **Quality** and **relevance** beat quantity.

---

## Revision checklist

- [ ] Build a **source table**: claim | evidence | strength | limitation.
- [ ] Practise **one** paragraph with **two** cited pieces of evidence.
- [ ] Check one statistic back to the **original** table or report.
