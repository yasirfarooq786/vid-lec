Programme: [Cambridge IGCSE Global Perspectives (0457)](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-igcse-global-perspectives-0457/).

---

## 1. What Global Perspectives is

- A skills-based course about **significant global issues** (environment, development, conflict, technology, rights, etc.—as your teacher frames them).
- You learn to move between **personal**, **local/national**, and **global** scales and to see **different viewpoints**.

---

## 2. “Global issue” in practice

- **Significance**: who is affected, how many, how deeply, for how long?
- **Interconnections**: economics, politics, culture, environment rarely stay separate.

---

## 3. Classroom habits that scale to assessment

- Keep a **running glossary** of issues and stakeholders.
- Practise turning a headline into a **question** you can research (not only an opinion).

---

## Common misconceptions

- “It is only opinion.” **Reasoned** judgement needs **evidence** and **criteria**.
- “Global means abroad only.” Your **local** experience can illustrate a global pattern.

---

## Revision checklist

- [ ] Write **one paragraph** defining a chosen issue in neutral terms.
- [ ] List **three** stakeholders with **different** interests.
- [ ] Identify **two** credible source types you would use first.
